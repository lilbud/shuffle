The big question in my mind coming out of [Night One in Philadelphia]({{< relref "2018-09-07-where-the-band-was-philadelphia-september-7-2016.md" >}}): how do you top a show that includes “New York City Serenade,” “[Lost in the Flood]({{< relref "2011-01-05-roll-of-the-dice-lost-in-the-flood.md" >}}/),” “[Kitty’s Back]({{< relref "2022-01-22-roll-of-the-dice-kittys-back.md" >}}), “[Incident on 57th Street]({{< relref "2018-12-14-roll-of-the-dice-incident-on-57th-street.md" >}}),” “[The Fever,]({{< relref "2020-10-04-roll-of-the-dice-the-fever.md" >}})” “[Thundercrack]({{< relref "2021-09-24-roll-of-the-dice-thundercrack.md" >}}), “[Downbound Train]({{< relref "1984-01-07-roll-of-the-dice-downbound-train.md" >}}),” “[Streets of Philadelphia]({{< relref "2021-06-30-roll-of-the-dice-streets-of-philadelphia.md" >}}),” and “Jungleland” on Night Two?

Or would this be the exception that disproves the Night One/Night Two truism?

Turns out you top it by keeping a couple of those gems, like the by-now standard “New York City Serenade” opener (that still feels so strange to write)…

{{< youtube UuDAUUy4Upg >}}

…swapping out the rest for genuine by-request wild cards (missing entirely from Night One) like “From Small Things (Big Things One Day Come),” “[I’m Goin’ Down]({{< relref "1984-01-11-roll-of-the-dice-im-goin-down.md" >}}),” and “[Loose Ends]({{< relref "2022-06-23-roll-of-the-dice-loose-end.md" >}})” (played in succession early in the set)…

{{< youtube SNKOQzAgFac >}}

{{< youtube cSZ3Kw5R2v4 >}}

…sharing the stage an old friend (even if he looked a little lost up there)…

{{< youtube cl3fbXl_Dxc >}}

…and a young fan (who couldn’t have seemed more at home)…

{{< youtube 76_gIYl2woo >}}

…and then performing what is easily the most exquisite “[Racing in the Street]({{< relref "2021-10-03-roll-of-the-dice-racing-in-the-street.md" >}})” I’ve ever had the privilege to witness.

{{< youtube DQc_HLYh_NI >}}

And it doesn’t hurt to restart my streak of getting at least one personal premiere per show going again, by playing my first “[Lucky Town]({{< relref "1992-02-03-roll-of-the-dice-lucky-town.md" >}}).”

{{< youtube yPk_tcXVVSs >}}

I only have one regret from this show: Bruce was slated to start the encore with “[Secret Garden]({{< relref "2019-05-30-roll-of-the-dice-secret-garden.md" >}})” which by this point was at the very top of my chase list (this tour had knocked off pretty much everything else), but for some reason Bruce decided to play “[Streets of Philadelphia]({{< relref "2021-06-30-roll-of-the-dice-streets-of-philadelphia.md" >}})” for a second night running.

{{< youtube qmqYhkmaCIE >}}

I saw six of the ten shows on this final leg of the tour, and yet I still managed to miss “[Secret Garden]({{< relref "2019-05-30-roll-of-the-dice-secret-garden.md" >}}),” which was played at 3 of the 4 shows I missed. But that’s a minor complaint, and one that seems ridiculous in light of a magnificent eight-song encore that also included “[Backstreets]({{< relref "2022-05-14-roll-of-the-dice-backstreets.md" >}})” and a show-closing “[Jersey Girl]({{< relref "1976-02-07-cover-me-roll-of-the-dice-jersey-girl.md" >}}/)” with fireworks.

{{< youtube kAE5yr3enQI >}}

And if the show didn’t set another longest-ever record, that was just fine, too: that night in Philly was one of the most humid I can remember at a show, and by the end of the night I was drenched and exhausted–it was Friday night, and I’d already seen three four-hour-ish shows that week without taking a single day off of work.

And I still had one more show to go that weekend–one that would be my last E Street show to date.
