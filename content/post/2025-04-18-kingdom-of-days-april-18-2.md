***New for 2025:** New entry for 1975 and 2024*<br>

**1970:** Steel Mill plays a gig at Ocean County College in Toms River, New Jersey.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/04/1970-04-18-rotated.jpg.webp)

**1973:** Bruce and his band play the Christian Brothers Academy in Lincroft, New Jersey, the first of only two high school gigs the band will ever perform.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/04/1973-0418.jpg.webp)

**1974:** Bruce and the band play a gig at Monmouth College in Long Branch, New Jersey.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/04/1974-04-18.jpg.webp)

**1975:** Bruce and his studio team move to the Record Plant in New York City. On this first day of recording, they work on "Thunder Road" and "Jungleland."<br>

**1981:** Bruce plays his first-ever show in France on this first of two nights at the Palais des Sports de Saint-Ouen in Paris. Another first: Bruce debuts his cover of "[Can't Help Falling in Love]({{< relref "1976-01-21-cover-me-cant-help-falling-in-love.md" >}}/)" at this show.<br>

{{< videopress id=wNB0ZUQS >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/04/1981-04-18-1.jpg.webp)

**1999:** TNT airs a Johnny Cash tribute special, on which Bruce performs "[Give My Love to Rose]({{< relref "2020-10-29-cover-me-give-my-love-to-rose.md" >}})."<br>

{{< youtube hEfe80Ty8KY >}}

**2000:** At their Reunion show in Houston, fans witness the E Street Band premiere of "[Better Days]({{< relref "1992-02-02-roll-of-the-dice-better-days.md" >}})."<br>

{{< videopress id=MuIILEbI >}}

**2003:** At the Corel Centre in Ottawa on the Rising Tour, Bruce plays "[I'm on Fire]({{< relref "1984-01-08-roll-of-the-dice-im-on-fire.md" >}})" for the only time on the tour. In the encores, Clarence collides with Bruce during "[Ramrod]({{< relref "2022-09-10-roll-of-the-dice-ramrod.md" >}})," falls on top of him, knocks them both to the ground, and they somehow manage to continue playing the entire time!<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/04/2003-04-18-rotated.jpg.webp)

**2004:** Patti plays two live shows to promote her upcoming *23rd Street Lullaby* album at The Hit Factory in New York City. Bruce provides backing vocals and guitar on "[Love (Stand Up)]({{< relref "2021-02-14-matr-patti-scialfa-and-bruce-springsteen-love-stand-up.md" >}})" and "[As Long As I (Can Be With You)]({{< relref "2018-10-02-meeting-across-the-river-patti-scialfa-and-bruce-springsteen-as-long-as-i-can-be-with-you.md" >}})." The initial run of the album will include a bonus disc with a live track from this show, but you can watch Patti's entire concert from that night below.<br>

{{< youtube WMG0Ji8Qqi4 >}}

**2007:** Bruce and Jesse Malin film the video for Jesse's single, "[Broken Radio]({{< relref "2019-08-17-matr-jesse-malin-and-bruce-springsteen-broken-radio.md" >}})."<br>

{{< youtube dlmBCrBJokA >}}

**2008:** Bruce reschedules a planned show in Sunrise, Florida due to the passing of Danny Federici.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/04/2008-04-18.jpg.webp)
