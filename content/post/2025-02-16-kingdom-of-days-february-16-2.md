**1964:** The Beatles make their second appearance on the Ed Sullivan show, and Bruce is watching.<br>

{{< youtube yqrYUORgY-s >}}

**1973:** Bruce and the band play Monmouth College in West Long Branch, New Jersey. Tickets are only one dollar if you bring along a newspaper to recycle.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/02/1973-02-16.png.webp)

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/02/1973-02-16.jpg.webp)

**1975:** *The Philadelphia Inquirer* does its best to wake the world beyond Philly and Texas to Springsteen’s power and talent. It won’t take much longer.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2024/02/The_Philadelphia_Inquirer_Sun__Feb_16__1975_-1-scaled.jpg)![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2024/02/The_Philadelphia_Inquirer_Sun__Feb_16__1975_-2-scaled.jpg.webp)

**1977:** Bruce and the E Street Band play a gig at Veterans Memorial Auditorium in Columbus.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/02/1977-0216.jpg.webp)

**1981**: Bruce and the boys play their second of two nights at the Lakeland Civic Center on the River Tour.<br>

{{< videopress id=dNaN6mOU >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/02/1981-02-16.png.webp)

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/02/1981-02-16.jpg.webp)

**1983:** At The Hit Factory in New York City, Bruce records “Refrigerator Blues,” which to this date does not circulate.<br>

**1985:** Bruce scores his first Number One album in the U.K. with *Born in the U.S.A*.<br>

**1991:** Bruce joins Bruce Willis, Stephen Stills, and John McEnroe(!) on “[C.C. Rider]({{< relref "2021-08-29-cover-me-c-c-rider.md" >}})” at the Tent on the Beach in Malibu.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/02/1991-02-16.png.webp)

**1997:** Bruce plays his second of three solo acoustic shows in Melbourne, Australia.<br>

{{< videopress id=yIuPpfhF >}}

**2012:** In Paris, Bruce is interviewed about his new *[Wrecking Ball]({{< relref "2023-03-28-album-companion-wrecking-ball.md" >}})* album by journalist Antoine de Caunes.<br>

{{< videopress id=BlkrjD6J >}}

**2014:** Night Two in Melbourne on the High Hopes Tour features a full album performance of *Born to Run* and tour debuts of “[Lucky Town]({{< relref "1992-02-03-roll-of-the-dice-lucky-town.md" >}}),” “[Roulette]({{< relref "2022-04-16-roll-of-the-dice-roulette.md" >}}),” “[Growin’ Up]({{< relref "2011-01-03-roll-of-the-dice-growin-up.md" >}}/),” [Lost in the Flood]({{< relref "2011-01-05-roll-of-the-dice-lost-in-the-flood.md" >}}/),” and “[Meeting Across the River]({{< relref "2019-08-04-roll-of-the-dice-meeting-across-the-river.md" >}}).” Curiously, no official recording is available of this show.<br>

**2017:** Night Two in Brisbane features the Australian premiere of “[Working on a Dream]({{< relref "2019-07-04-roll-of-the-dice-working-on-a-dream.md" >}}),” rare outings for “[Roll of the Dice]({{< relref "2020-01-18-roll-of-the-dice-roll-of-the-dice.md" >}}),” “[Jole Blon]({{< relref "2019-08-29-matr-gary-u-s-bonds-and-bruce-springsteen-jole-blon.md" >}}),” and “Follow that Dream,” and a guest audience member for “[Growin’ Up.]({{< relref "2011-01-03-roll-of-the-dice-growin-up.md" >}}/)” Also tour premiering tonight: “[Long Time Comin’]({{< relref "2022-06-19-roll-of-the-dice-long-time-comin.md" >}})” and “[Fire]({{< relref "2020-08-05-roll-of-the-dice-fire.md" >}}).”<br>

{{< videopress id=Y4yBWysX >}}

**2023:** COVID-19 continues its tour across the E Street Band, moving on from Nils (who returns to the stage) and taking temporary residence in Jake. E Street Horns saxophonist Ed Manion steps up for double-duty, and the band soldiers on in Austin. “[Cadillac Ranch]({{< relref "2020-07-23-roll-of-the-dice-cadillac-ranch.md" >}})” makes its tour premiere tonight. “[Don’t Play That Song]({{< relref "2023-02-12-cover-me-dont-play-that-song-you-lied.md" >}}),” an early highlight, is dropped from the set and won’t appear again.<br>
