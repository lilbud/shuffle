***New for 2025:** Audio for 2005*<br>

**1972:** Bruce plays his fifth of six two-shows-a-night solo gigs at Max's Kansas City this week.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2019/08/1972-08-11.jpg.webp)

**1974:** Bruce cancels a gig at the Cambridge Performance Center to make room for *Born to Run* recording sessions. Bruce does play a show that evening at the Stone Balloon in Newark, though–his one and only show ever in the state of Delaware.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/08/1974-0813.jpg.webp)

**1975:** Bruce's legendary stand at the Bottom Line in New York City begins here, on Night One of a five-night/ten-show residency, during which 25% of all tickets are distributed by Columbia Records to journalists and employees, establishing high stakes for Bruce and the E Street Band throughout the stand. The early show is mostly undocumented but known to feature Bruce's first performance of "[When You Walk in the Room]({{< relref "2020-10-08-cover-me-when-you-walk-in-the-room.md" >}})" and only the second-ever performance of "[Backstreets]({{< relref "2022-05-14-roll-of-the-dice-backstreets.md" >}})." The late show features the first ever performance of "[Night]({{< relref "2019-10-31-roll-of-the-dice-night.md" >}})" and the last recorded "New York City Serenade" until the Reunion Tour almost a quarter-century later.<br>
