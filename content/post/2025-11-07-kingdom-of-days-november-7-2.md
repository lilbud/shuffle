***New for 2025:** New entry for 1977; audio for 2006*<br>

**1973:** Daryl Hall and John Oates open for Bruce and the E Street Band for two shows at Max's Kansas City in New York. This is the second of a five-night/ten-show stand.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2019/10/1973-11-06.jpg.webp)

**1974:** Bruce and the E Street Band play their second of two nights at Armadillo World Headquarters in Austin.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2019/10/1974-11-07.jpg.webp)

**1976:** Songwriter Carl D'Errico (co-writer of "[It's My Life]({{< relref "2021-11-23-cover-me-its-my-life.md" >}})," which Bruce performed a few days prior at the Palladium, with D'Errico in attendance) invites Bruce over to his house for dinner. Bruce accepts, dines, and jams with D'Errico that evening.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/11/1976-1107.jpg.webp)

**1977:** Bruce holds a recording session for *Darkness on the Edge of Town* at The Record Plant in New York City.<br>

**1978:** Bruce plays the first rock concert at Cornell University ever to have reserved seat ticketing. During the show, Bruce debuts his "I Get Mad" interlude in "[She's the One]({{< relref "2021-04-04-roll-of-the-dice-shes-the-one.md" >}})." The sound quality isn't great, but you can listen to it below.<br>

**1987:** *[Tunnel of Love]({{< relref "1987-01-01-album-companion-tunnel-of-love.md" >}})*reaches the Number 1 spot on the album charts in the U.S.<br>

**2006:** Bruce premieres a new arrangement of "[Blinded by the Light]({{< relref "2011-01-02-roll-of-the-dice-blinded-by-the-light.md" >}})" to open his Seeger Sessions show in Antwerp.<br>

{{< youtube 7VTKGHf6OqM >}}

{{< youtube g6BjFSg3s2M >}}

**2007:** At Town Hall in New York City, Bruce supports Stand Up for Heroes, a benefit show for the Bob Woodruff Family Fund, sponsored by the New York Comedy Festival. Bruce plays three acoustic songs and auctions his motorcycle to support the foundation.<br>

{{< youtube QFA1QSoiF6E >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/11/2007-11-07.png.webp)

**2009:** Bruce and the E Street Band play the first of two very special full-album shows at Madison Square Garden in New York City. Tonight, Night One, features Bruce's first full performance of *The Wild, The Innocent & The E Street Shuffle*, featuring the tour premieres of "[Wild Billy's Circus Story]({{< relref "2019-03-14-roll-of-the-dice-wild-billys-circus-story.md" >}})" and "New York City Serenade." The band is joined by full horn and string sections for the occasion, plus Richard Blackwell on conga, and Elvis Costello makes a guest appearance in the encores for "[(Your Love Keeps Lifting Me) Higher and Higher]({{< relref "2018-09-23-cover-me-your-love-keeps-lifting-me-higher-and-higher.md" >}})." This show is available as part of Bruce's official archive series–get it here.<br>

**2016:** On Election Eve, Bruce supports U.S. presidential candidate Hillary Clinton, performing three acoustic songs at a rally at Independence Hall in Philadelphia.<br>

{{< youtube Jsf5w6w1QSs >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/11/2016-11-07.png.webp)

**2017:** *Springsteen on Broadway* continues its theatrical run at the Walter Kerr Theater in New York City.<br>

**2018:** *Springsteen on Broadway* continues its theatrical run at the Walter Kerr Theater in New York City. In attendance tonight: John Fogerty.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2019/10/2018-11-07.jpg.webp)

**2020:** Bruce appears in the introduction video at Jon Landau's induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.<br>

{{< videopress id=0OS8Z512 >}}

**2022:** Bruce makes his customary appearance at Stand Up For Heroes at David Geffen Hall in New York City. His four-song set features the public debut of "[House of a Thousand Guitars]({{< relref "2016-01-15-roll-of-the-dice-house-of-a-thousand-guitars.md" >}})."<br>
