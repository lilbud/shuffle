***New for 2025:** No updates*<br>

**1974:** Bruce and the E Street Band co-headline with Roger McGuinn at The Troubadour in West Hollywood, a surprise gig that only became public three days prior. (Bruce doesn't actually take the stage until almost 2am, however, so this technically qualifies as an entry for July 31.)<br>

**1975:** Bruce wraps up a three-night stand at the Carter Barron Amphitheater in Washington DC's Rock Creek Park, where he debuts his cover of The Drifters' "[Up on the Roof]({{< relref "2018-12-15-cover-me-up-on-the-roof.md" >}})."<br>

**1985:** Bruce and the band rehearse for the final leg of the Born in the U.S.A. Tour at the former home of Big Man's West in Red Bank.<br>

**1987:** Bruce makes a guest appearance with Jah Love at Key Largo in Belmar, New Jersey, playing reggae versions of "[Born in the U.S.A.]({{< relref "1984-01-03-roll-of-the-dice-born-in-the-u-s-a.md" >}})" and "[My Hometown]({{< relref "1983-01-12-roll-of-the-dice-my-hometown.md" >}})," along with covers of "[One Love]({{< relref "2019-11-28-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-jah-love-one-love-people-get-ready.md" >}})" and "[People Get Ready.]({{< relref "2019-01-06-matr-u2-bruce-springsteen-and-patti-scialfa-people-get-ready.md" >}})"<br>

**1988:** The Tunnel of Love Tour nears its end with this final German show in Bremen, where "[Working on the Highway]({{< relref "1984-01-06-roll-of-the-dice-working-on-the-highway.md" >}})" makes its tour debut.<br>

**1992:** At Night Five of eleven at World Tour 1992's opening stand in the Meadowlands, Bruce performs "[All the Way Home]({{< relref "2019-09-08-roll-of-the-dice-all-the-way-home.md" >}})" for the first time, and "[4th of July, Asbury Park (Sandy)]({{< relref "2019-04-26-roll-of-the-dice-4th-of-july-asbury-park-sandy.md" >}})" comes out as well, accompanied by only Bruce and Roy.<br>

**2002:** Finally! After 18 years, Bruce releases a new E Street Band album. *The Rising* is an instant success, the darling of music reviewers and critics, and the catalyst for a new era that lasts to this day, cementing the return of the E Street Band. To celebrate the occasion, Bruce and the band get up early to appear on NBC's *Today Show* for a mini-concert. Katie Couric interviews virtually the entire band (poor Garry!) before the show. Watch that fun interview below, followed by the songs performed that morning.<br>

**2007:** Bruce's close friend, personal assistant, and right-hand man Terry Magovern passes away at the age of 67. Terry's life and death will inspire Bruce to write "[Terry's Song]({{< relref "2018-07-30-roll-of-the-dice-terrys-song.md" >}})."<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/07/2007-07-30.png.webp)
