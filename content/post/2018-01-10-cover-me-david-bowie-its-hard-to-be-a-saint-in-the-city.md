Two years gone to the day, but his music lives on. Enjoy one of the earliest covers of a Bruce Springsteen song (and a darn good one): David Bowie's cover of "[It's Hard to Be a Saint in the City.]({{< relref "2011-01-09-roll-of-the-dice-its-hard-to-be-a-saint-in-the-city.md" >}}/)"

{{< youtube _pDYK4Ns-co >}}

Bowie recorded this in 1974, but it languished in the vaults until he released it on his *Sound + Vision* box set in 1989.

Although Bowie recorded this very early in Bruce's career, it was actually the second time he'd recorded one of Bruce's songs. ([The first]({{< relref "2011-02-01-cover-me-david-bowie-growin-up.md" >}}/) was "[Growin' Up.]({{< relref "2011-01-03-roll-of-the-dice-growin-up.md" >}}/)")

And the two icons hadn't even met yet! For the story of that first meeting, see [that day's Kingdom of Days entry]({{< relref "2025-11-25-kingdom-of-days-november-25-2.md" >}}/).
