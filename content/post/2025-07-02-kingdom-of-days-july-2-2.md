***New for 2025:** New entry for 1977 and 2024; video for 2023*<br>

**1949:** "Professor" Roy Bittan is born in Rockaway Beach, New York.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/07/roy.jpeg.webp)

**1966:** The Castiles play an opening set for The Rogues at the new Surf 'n' See Club in Sea Bright, New Jersey.<br>

**1967:** The "Fabulous" Castiles play a gig at the See 'n' Surf Club (not a typo–same club, new name) to mark its first anniversary.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/07/1967-0702.jpg.webp)

**1971:** The Sundance Blues Band plays the Upstage in Asbury Park.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/07/1971-07-02.jpg.webp)

**1976:** Mike Appel sends Bruce a letter informing him that he is prohibited from recording new music due to their ongoing legal dispute. Bruce will spend 1976 and much of 1977 touring instead of recording as a result.<br>

**1977:** Bruce and the E Street Band record "[Don't Look Back]({{< relref "2020-05-03-roll-of-the-dice-dont-look-back.md" >}})" at Atlantic Studios in New York City, and Bruce works on a beautiful early solo piano arrangement of "[Racing in the Street]({{< relref "2021-10-03-roll-of-the-dice-racing-in-the-street.md" >}})."<br>

{{< youtube pvMn2zFVX0E >}}

**1981:** Bruce and the E Street Band kick off the final leg of the River Tour by playing the first-ever event (by anyone) at the Meadowlands. Bruce debuts his cover of "[Jersey Girl]({{< relref "1976-02-07-cover-me-roll-of-the-dice-jersey-girl.md" >}}/)" for the occasion; other highlights include the tour premiere of "[I Don't Want to Go Home]({{< relref "2018-03-30-meeting-across-the-river-bruce-springsteen-and-steve-van-zandt-i-dont-want-to-go-home.md" >}})" and the last-ever "Born to Run" opener.<br>

**1984:** The last of three nights in St. Paul (and the opening stand of the Born in the U.S.A. Tour) features the live debuts of "[Downbound Train]({{< relref "1984-01-07-roll-of-the-dice-downbound-train.md" >}})" and "[Cover Me]({{< relref "1984-01-04-roll-of-the-dice-cover-me.md" >}})," along with the last full-band arrangement of "[No Surrender]({{< relref "1984-01-09-roll-of-the-dice-no-surrender.md" >}})" for fifteen years.<br>

{{< videopress id=PWy5WzaZ >}}

**1986:** On the CBS television network, *West 57th* airs a segment profiling Clarence.<br>

{{< youtube 3QvN4MwV3wk >}}

**1988:** The Tunnel of Love Tour comes to Stockholm for the first of two nights. The second night will make headlines, but Night One still provides a great show, featuring the tour premiere of "[Cadillac Ranch]({{< relref "2020-07-23-roll-of-the-dice-cadillac-ranch.md" >}})."<br>

{{< youtube wBU8DiGV6j0 >}}

**1989:** Bruce joins Gary U.S. Bonds at The Cafe Bar in Long Branch for six songs: "[This Little Girl,]({{< relref "2018-09-06-rotd-matr-cover-me-gary-u-s-bonds-bruce-springsteen-the-e-street-band-this-little-girl.md" >}})" "[Angelyne]({{< relref "2019-02-04-cover-me-gary-u-s-bonds-and-the-e-street-band-angelyne.md" >}})," "[Bony Moronie](https://youtu.be/3QvN4MwV3wk)," "[Your Love]({{< relref "2018-07-27-cover-me-gary-u-s-bonds-your-love.md" >}})," "[Dedication,]({{< relref "2018-06-17-cover-me-roll-of-the-dice-gary-u-s-bonds-dedication.md" >}})" and "[New Orleans](../2019-07-31-cover-me-new-orleans/post.md/)."<br>

**1992:** A scheduled show in Barcelona is postponed by two days due to a transport worker strike.<br>

**1999:** Bruce films a cameo performance in the movie *High Fidelity* at Disney Soundstage Studios in New York City.<br>

{{< videopress id=e9i42Z2L >}}

**2009:** The Working on a Dream Tour arrives in Germany. In Munich, Bruce plays back-to-back tour premieres of "This Hard Land" and "[Oh, Pretty Woman]({{< relref "2019-08-25-matr-roy-orbison-and-bruce-springsteen-oh-pretty-woman.md" >}})."<br>

**2023:** "Downbound Train makes its tour debut when Bruce and the E Street Band play their second of two shows in Oslo.<br>
