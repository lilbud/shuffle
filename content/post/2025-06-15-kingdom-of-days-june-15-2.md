***New for 2025:** New entry for 1979*<br>

**1973:** At Madison Square Garden, Bruce and his band play their thirteenth and final show as the opening act for Chicago. This was a notoriously bad tour for the band, playing to huge but unreceptive (and sometimes booing) crowds. Bruce resolves never to play big halls again, and he sticks to it... for a while.<br>

**1974:** Under record label pressure to release a new single, Bruce and the E Street Band cancel a show at Armadillo World Headquarters in Austin.<br>

**1979:** Bruce and the E Street Band work on "[The Price You Pay]({{< relref "2019-09-23-roll-of-the-dice-the-price-you-pay.md" >}})" at The Power Station in New York City.<br>

**1981:** Bruce joins Gary U.S. Bonds on stage at Old Waldorf in San Francisco, performing "[This Little Girl]({{< relref "2018-09-06-rotd-matr-cover-me-gary-u-s-bonds-bruce-springsteen-the-e-street-band-this-little-girl.md" >}})" for the first time ever along with his one and only performance of "[School Is Out]({{< relref "2018-09-29-meeting-across-the-river-gary-u-s-bonds-and-bruce-springsteen-school-is-out.md" >}})."<br>

**1983:** At The Hit Factory in New York City, Bruce records "[Cynthia]({{< relref "2019-09-06-roll-of-the-dice-cynthia.md" >}})" and "[Don't Back Down.]({{< relref "1983-01-14-roll-of-the-dice-dont-back-down.md" >}})" The former won't be released for another fifteen years; the latter is unreleased to this day.<br>
