**New for 2025:** No updates<br>

**1975:** Bruce drops by the Bottom Line in New York City to catch Patti Smith's late show, providing backing vocals for "[Gloria]({{< relref "2018-03-16-cover-me-gloria.md" >}})."<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/12/1975-1226.jpg.webp)

**1995:** Bruce announces an extension of his solo acoustic tour in the new year, with select dates in North America before heading overseas.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/12/1995-12-2t6.jpg.webp)

**2017:** Patti Scialfa is sick with the flu and cannot perform, throwing a monkey wrench into the *Springsteen on Broadway* storyline and set list. Bruce adapts by changing that part of the show to focus on his kids instead of his wife, and accordingly, "[Long Time Comin']({{< relref "2022-06-19-roll-of-the-dice-long-time-comin.md" >}})" makes its first appearance in the show, in the spot where "[Tougher Than the Rest]({{< relref "1987-01-02-roll-of-the-dice-tougher-than-the-rest.md" >}}/)" would normally be. Bruce also swaps out "[Brilliant Disguise]({{< relref "1987-01-09-roll-of-the-dice-brilliant-disguise.md" >}}/)" for "[The Ghost of Tom Joad]({{< relref "2019-10-17-roll-of-the-dice-the-ghost-of-tom-joad.md" >}})," giving added weight to the segment on his social values.<br>
