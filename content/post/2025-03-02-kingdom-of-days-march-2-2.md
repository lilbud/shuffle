**1974:** Bruce cancels a show in Parsippany, New Jersey due to illness.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2025/03/The_Record_1974_03_01_27-scaled.jpg.webp)

**1975:** Another cancellation, this one in Syracuse, due to a conflict with mixing sessions for *Born to Run*.<br>

**1977:** The Lawsuit Tour makes its way to Atlanta's Civic Center Auditorium.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/02/1977-03-02.jpg.webp)

**1981:** The River Tour moves on to the Hampton Roads Coliseum in Hampton, Virginia.<br>

{{< videopress id=1tSamoBy >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/03/1981-03-02.jpg.webp)

**1986:** A rare "E Street Sneak Attack" as the entire band (minus Roy) plays a set at The Stone Pony, where standard songs are given new life with extended jams and solos.<br>

{{< videopress id=cfkCcj42 >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/03/1986-03-02.jpg.webp)

**1988:** Bruce wins the Best Rock Vocal Performance (Solo) Grammy Award for "[Tunnel of Love.]({{< relref "1987-01-07-roll-of-the-dice-tunnel-of-love.md" >}}/)" Although he's also nominated for Best Pop Vocal Performance (Male) for "[Brilliant Disguise]({{< relref "1987-01-09-roll-of-the-dice-brilliant-disguise.md" >}}/)" and Best Rock Instrumental Performance (Orchestra, Group or Soloist) for "[Paradise by the 'C']({{< relref "2020-12-12-roll-of-the-dice-paradise-by-the-c.md" >}})," he fails to win either.<br>

**2003:** Joe Ely joins the E Street Band for "[All Just to Get to You]({{< relref "2022-02-23-matr-joe-ely-and-bruce-springsteen-all-just-to-get-to-you.md" >}})" and "[Working on the Highway]({{< relref "1984-01-06-roll-of-the-dice-working-on-the-highway.md" >}})" during the Austin stop on the Rising Tour. This show also features the tour premiere of "[War]({{< relref "2016-01-02-cover-me-war.md" >}})" as the U.S. prepares for war with Iraq.<br>

**2008:** The Magic Tour begins its Canadian leg with a show at Bell Center in Montreal. A local reviewer informs us that the sax solo on "[Livin' in the Future]({{< relref "2023-06-24-roll-of-the-dice-livin-in-the-future.md" >}})" was played by Clarence Thomas... what a surreal guest appearance that must have been.<br>

**2012:** Bruce is a guest on *Late Night with Jimmy Fallon*, where he pokes fun of himself by dressing as his *Born in the U.S.A.*-era self for a parody of "[Sexy and I Know It]({{< relref "2018-03-24-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-jimmy-fallon-sexy-and-i-know-it.md" >}})." Later Bruce and the band (plus Tom Morello) premiere "[Death to My Hometown]({{< relref "2016-01-04-roll-of-the-dice-death-to-my-hometown.md" >}})" and "[Jack of All Trades]({{< relref "2020-11-27-roll-of-the-dice-jack-of-all-trades.md" >}})" and play a rare "[The E Street Shuffle]({{< relref "2020-06-06-roll-of-the-dice-the-e-street-shuffle.md" >}})." **2014:** Night two in Auckland, New Zealand again features an acoustic cover of "[Royals]({{< relref "2020-04-25-cover-me-royals.md" >}})" to open, along with another full album performance–this time *Born to Run*.<br>

**2018:** *Springsteen on Broadway* continues its theatrical run at the Walter Kerr Theater in New York City.<br>

**2023:** For the first time since the tour's opening week, the entire E Street Band is on stage and COVID-free, as Bruce's 2023 World Tour makes a stop at Ball Arena in Denver. Earlier in the day, Bruce stops into a Peruvian goods shop in Larimer Square.<br>
