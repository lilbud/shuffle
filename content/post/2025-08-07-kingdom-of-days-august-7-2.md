***New for 2025:** Expanded entry for 1973; new entry for 2024*<br>

**1971:** The Bruce Springsteen Band headlines at Asbury Park's Sunshine In.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/07/1971-08-07.png.webp)

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/08/1971-0807.jpg.webp)

**1973:** A scheduled show at Uncle Al's Erlton Theatre Lounge in Cherry Hill is likely postponed (no record exists of this show having taken place) to the following week. Instead, Bruce works on overdubs and mixing for "[Thundercrack]({{< relref "2021-09-24-roll-of-the-dice-thundercrack.md" >}})," "New York City Serenade," and "Zero and Blind Terry."<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2024/08/Courier_Post_1973_07_21_Page_25-scaled.jpg.webp)

**1976:** Bruce and the E Street Band wrap up their six-night stand at the Monmouth Arts Center in Red Bank, New Jersey. Clips from the show can be seen in the box set and documentary, *The Promise: The Making of Darkness on the Edge of Town*.<br>

{{< youtube ioQ2eF1XbYY >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/08/1976-0807.jpg.webp)

**1978:** The Darkness Tour stop in Kalamazoo underperforms in ticket sales, with over half of the seats at Wings Stadium going unoccupied.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/08/1978-0807.jpg.webp)

**1981:** Bruce and the E Street Band wrap up their three-night stand at the Capital Center in Largo, Maryland.<br>

{{< videopress id=0KQVFFpL >}}

**1982:** Bruce joins Beaver Brown on stage at Big Man's West in Red Bank, where he debuts a work-in-progress, "[Downbound Train]({{< relref "1984-01-07-roll-of-the-dice-downbound-train.md" >}})" in the middle of "[Lucille]({{< relref "1976-01-07-cover-me-lucille.md" >}}/)." (Skip to the 21 minute-mark below to hear it.)<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/08/1982-08-07-scaled.jpg.webp)

**1984:** *Born in the U.S.A.* is certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America.<br>

**1992:** At the penultimate show of World Tour 1992's eleven-night stand at the Meadowlands, Bruce and Roy play a tour one-off, an intimate "[For You]({{< relref "2011-01-07-roll-of-the-dice-for-you.md" >}})." "[Racing in the Street]({{< relref "2021-10-03-roll-of-the-dice-racing-in-the-street.md" >}})" is also a tour one-off, and Southside Johnny guest-stars on "[All the Way Home]({{< relref "2019-09-08-roll-of-the-dice-all-the-way-home.md" >}})."<br>

**1999:** Bruce stuns fans at the Meadowlands when he opens Night Twelve (of fifteen) with the first "[Don't Look Back]({{< relref "2020-05-03-roll-of-the-dice-dont-look-back.md" >}})" in 22 years. Bruce also plays "[Janey, Don't You Lose Heart]({{< relref "2019-04-06-roll-of-the-dice-janey-dont-you-lose-heart.md" >}})" for only the third time ever.<br>

{{< videopress id=MBTGYj28 >}}

**2002:** The Rising Tour officially kicks off and Soozie Tyrell makes her official E Street Band debut at the Continental Airlines Arena in East Rutherford, with essentially the same set as the rehearsal show in the same venue. Early shows on the "barnstorming" leg would be atypically rigid, with little variation from night to night.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/08/2002-0807.jpg.webp)

**2005:** Bruce opens his solo acoustic show at Milwaukee's Bradley Center with the tour premiere (and tour one-off) of "[Shut Out the Light]({{< relref "2020-10-14-roll-of-the-dice-shut-out-the-light.md" >}})" and follow up shortly thereafter with the tour premiere of "[Frankie]({{< relref "2019-03-31-roll-of-the-dice-frankie.md" >}})."<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/07/2005-08-07b.png.webp)

**2019:** Bruce attends the premiere of *Blinded by the Light* at Convention Hall in Asbury Park. Following the show, Bruce, Patti, and Lisa Lowell join Southside Johnny and The Asbury Jukes on stage at the after-party for four songs, including "[Talk to Me]({{< relref "2019-02-26-roll-of-the-dice-talk-to-me.md" >}})" and "[Sherry Darling]({{< relref "2018-07-06-roll-of-the-dice-sherry-darling.md" >}})."<br>
