***New for 2025:** Video for 1999 and 2023; new entry for 2022*<br>

**1971:** The Bruce Springsteen Band kicks off a residency at the Student Prince in Asbury Park that will last the rest of the calendar year.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/09/1971-0903.jpg.webp)

**1999:** Bruce and the reunited E Street Band wrap up their three-night stand at the MCI Center in Washington, DC with an all-star guest-star line-up: Bonnie Raitt, Bruce Hornsby, Jackson Browne, Shawn Colvin, Mary Chapin Carpenter and Frank Bruno join the band for the encores.<br>

**2001:** In their last public appearance before the nation changes forever, Bruce and Patti join Brian Kirk and the Jirks on-stage for four songs at Donovan's Reef in Sea Bright, New Jersey. (The Jirks are the house band at the club.)<br>

**2012:** Night Two at Philadelphia's Citizens Bank Park was a Labor Day special, opening with an acoustic tour premiere of "[Factory]({{< relref "2018-10-31-roll-of-the-dice-factory.md" >}})" as part of a five-song [*Darkness on the Edge of Town*]({{< relref "2022-07-05-album-companion-darkness-on-the-edge-of-town.md" >}})mini-set. The show stayed intense all the way through, which may be why Bruce omitted setlisted gems like "[Ain't Good Enough for You]({{< relref "2019-06-02-roll-of-the-dice-aint-good-enough-for-you.md" >}})," "Love Train," "[(Your Love Keeps Lifting Me) Higher and Higher]({{< relref "2018-09-23-cover-me-your-love-keeps-lifting-me-higher-and-higher.md" >}})," "[Incident on 57th Street]({{< relref "2018-12-14-roll-of-the-dice-incident-on-57th-street.md" >}})," and "[My Love Will Not Let You Down]({{< relref "2018-01-24-roll-of-the-dice-my-love-will-not-let-you-down.md" >}})." What a show *that* would have been!<br>

**2016:** A scheduled River Tour show in Virginia Beach is postponed for two days due to Tropical Storm Hermine.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/09/2016-0903.jpg.webp)

**2021:** It's the penultimate night for *Springsteen on Broadway* at the St. James Theater in New York City.<br>

**2022:** Bruce records "Addicted to Romance" in his home studio in Colts Neck.<br>

{{< youtube FPM0gG2ueV8 >}}

**2023:** Featuring the tour premiere of "[Two Hearts]({{< relref "2018-05-28-roll-of-the-dice-two-hearts.md" >}})" and rarities like "Jungleland" and "[Jersey Gir]({{< relref "1976-02-07-cover-me-roll-of-the-dice-jersey-girl.md" >}}/)l," the third and final show at MetLife Stadium seems to promise a looser setlist in the autumn leg of World Tour 2023. Sadly, it proves to be the E Street Band's last show of the year.<br>
