I gotta envy the Australians, because watching this video made my heart leap into my throat.

{{< youtube vAYL_oI_bHA >}}

This was six days ago, folks: live music in a packed club. Sigh... someday, that'll be us too.

In the meantime, though, we can live vicariously thanks to The Album Show, a "live performance concept" that brings classic albums to new life on the stage.

Led by Sydney-based Michael Carpenter, The Album Show shone their spotlight on Bruce Springsteen's *Born in the U.S.A.* last week, and they generously filmed and shared highlights from that performance.

All of their selections are great, but I'm particularly fond of their interpretation of "[Cover Me,]({{< relref "1984-01-04-roll-of-the-dice-cover-me.md" >}})" a song I don't even like in its original version. The Album Show's version is smokier and more nocturnal than Bruce's original, and it's a perfect fit for the club's ambience.

Here's hoping they release the whole show. In the meantime, check out more of their covers of classic albums [here](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCextdy0IL2N7w3ktIolAzZA/videos?view=0&sort=dd&shelf_id=0).
