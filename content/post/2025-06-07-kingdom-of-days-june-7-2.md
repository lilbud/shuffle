***New for 2025:** Audio for 1972; review for 1973; new entry for 1977*<br>

**1969:** Child headlines at the eighth grade graduation concert at Blessed Sacrament Regional School in Margate, New Jersey.<br>

**1972:** Recording sessions begin for Bruce's first album, *[Greetings From Asbury Park, N.J.]({{< relref "2023-01-05-album-companion-greetings-from-asbury-park-n-j.md" >}})*Bruce records five solo tracks today: "[Arabian Nights,]({{< relref "2011-01-11-roll-of-the-dice-arabian-nights.md" >}})" "[Cowboys of the Sea]({{< relref "2011-01-15-roll-of-the-dice-cowboys-of-the-sea.md" >}})," "[Growin' Up,]({{< relref "2011-01-03-roll-of-the-dice-growin-up.md" >}}/)" "[Lady and the Doctor]({{< relref "2011-01-21-roll-of-the-dice-lady-and-the-doctor.md" >}}/)," and "[Street Queen]({{< relref "2011-01-28-roll-of-the-dice-street-queen.md" >}}/)." Only "Growin' Up" will eventually be released, and even that song will be re-recorded later in the month before being selected for the album.<br>

{{< videopress id=IvoYACpv >}}

**1973:** Bruce and the E Street Band open for Chicago at the Onondaga County War Memorial Auditorium in Syracuse, New York. The local paper mis-identifies Bruce, and they aren't even close: somehow, they refer to him as "Bris Christy." Mr. Reschke didn't do so well with the song titles, either. See if you can figure out the songs Bruce actually played below.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2025/06/Syracuse_Herald_Journal_1973_06_08_16-scaled.jpg.webp)

**1976:** Bruce and most of the E Street Band join Southside Johnny and The Asbury Jukes on stage at a private party for *Crawdaddy* magazine.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/06/1976-0612.jpg.webp)

**1977:** Bruce and the band work on "[Don't Look Back]({{< relref "2020-05-03-roll-of-the-dice-dont-look-back.md" >}})" at Atlantic Studios in New York City.<br>

**1981:** In Birmingham, Pete Townshend makes a guest appearance in the encores of the first show of the last European stand of the River Tour. In the main set, Bruce performs "[Drive All Night]({{< relref "2018-02-27-roll-of-the-dice-drive-all-night.md" >}})" for the last time for 24 years.<br>
