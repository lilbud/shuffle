So this is a thing: Iconic TV stars from the 1970s covering songs by Bruce Springsteen. Sure, it’s a micro-genre, but a surprisingly well-represented one.

So far on the Shuffle, we’ve featured Tom “Luke Duke” Wopat’s [wonderfully atmospheric cover]({{< relref "2019-04-17-cover-me-tom-wopat-meeting-across-the-river.md" >}}) of “[Meeting Across the River]({{< relref "2019-08-04-roll-of-the-dice-meeting-across-the-river.md" >}})” and Suzi “Leather Tuscadero” Quatro’s [terrific version]({{< relref "2019-03-16-cover-me-suzi-quatro-born-to-run.md" >}}) of “Born to Run.”

Here’s installment #3: Lynda “Wonder Woman” Carter’s gentle country arrangement of “[I’m on Fire]({{< relref "1984-01-08-roll-of-the-dice-im-on-fire.md" >}}).”

{{< youtube K86HGZQ4i4I >}}

Can’t say I’m a fan of this particular arrangement–I think it entirely loses the heat at the heart of the song–but it’s a valiant attempt, and hey, it’s Wonder Woman.

Bonus: here’s a snippet of Lynda performing the song live. It’s only a minute, but it’s the only clip I could find.
