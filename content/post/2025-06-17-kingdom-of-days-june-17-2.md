***New for 2025:** New entry for 1977 and 2024; video for 2012 and 2016*<br>

**1972:** Bruce and Clarence play together for only the second time ever when Bruce makes a guest appearance with Clarence's band, Norman Seldin and the Joyful Noyze, at The Shipbottom Lounge in Point Pleasant Beach, New Jersey.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2019/06/joyful.jpg.webp)

**1973:** A scheduled gig in Chester, West Virginia, is believed to have been cancelled.<br>

**1977:** At Atlantic Studios in New York City, Bruce and the E Street Band record for their upcoming album, *Darkness on the Edge of Town*. Listen to their early cuts of "[One Way Street]({{< relref "2019-01-14-roll-of-the-dice-one-way-street.md" >}})" and "[Fire]({{< relref "2020-08-05-roll-of-the-dice-fire.md" >}})" (neither of which will make the album) from this date below.<br>

{{< youtube iB7xRtFOrZ8 >}}

**1978:** Bruce and the E Street Band play to a half-sold Kiel Auditorium in St. Louis on the Darkness Tour. In attendance is 18-year-old Michael Stipe, who will share a stage with Bruce decades later when he fronts the rock band R.E.M.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/06/1978-06-17.jpg.webp)

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/06/1978-0617.jpg.webp)

**1992:** At Night Two in Stockholm and for World Tour 1992 itself, Bruce plays "[All or Nothin' at All]({{< relref "2018-01-30-roll-of-the-dice-all-or-nothing-at-all.md" >}})" and "[Real Man]({{< relref "2020-04-15-roll-of-the-dice-real-man.md" >}})" for the first time.<br>

**1999:** "[I'm on Fire]({{< relref "1984-01-08-roll-of-the-dice-im-on-fire.md" >}})" makes its Reunion Tour debut when Bruce and the E Street Band make a Reunion Tour stop at Weserstadion in Bremen, Germany. During the pre-show soundcheck, Bruce plays the first known performance of "[Waitin' on a Sunny Day]({{< relref "2019-09-27-roll-of-the-dice-waitin-on-a-sunny-day.md" >}})."<br>

**2000:** At Night Three of ten at the Reunion Tour-closing Madison Square Garden stand, Bruce and the band play "[The E Street Shuffle]({{< relref "2020-06-06-roll-of-the-dice-the-e-street-shuffle.md" >}})" for the first time in 25 years. (Bruce seems to have forgotten he'd ever played it at all.) Bruce opens the show with his third-ever performance of "[Code of Silence]({{< relref "2018-08-27-codeofsilence.md" >}})."<br>

{{< youtube lVdLGGyqm8I >}}

**2003:** Night Two in Helsinki on the Rising Tour. "[You're Missing]({{< relref "2020-02-11-roll-of-the-dice-youre-missing.md" >}})" drops out of the set list for the first time, replaced by "[The River]({{< relref "2021-03-04-roll-of-the-dice-the-river.md" >}})," as the tour set list begins its customary loosening toward its final months.<br>

{{< videopress id=9SQx7riV >}}

**2004:** Promoting her new album, *23rd Street Lullaby*, Patti Scialfa makes a guest appearance and performance on *The View*.<br>

{{< videopress id=5vc82MI0 >}}

**2006:** Bruce and the Sessions Band make a tour stop at the DTE Energy Music Theater in Clarkston, Michigan. The performance of "[Bring 'Em Home]({{< relref "2006-01-18-rotd-cover-me-bring-em-home.md" >}})" on the *American Land* edition of [*We Shall Overcome*]({{< relref "2006-01-01-album-companion-we-shall-overcome.md" >}}) was recorded at this show, as was the official video for "[Pay Me My Money Down]({{< relref "2006-01-12-roll-of-the-dice-cover-me-pay-me-my-money-down.md" >}})."<br>

{{< youtube BYF8IntUFZI >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/06/2006-06-17.jpg.webp)

**2012:** Bruce brings the Wrecking Ball Tour to Madrid and plays "[Spanish Eyes]({{< relref "2023-01-14-roll-of-the-dice-spanish-eyes.md" >}})" for the very first time. Southside Johnny is in town and joins Bruce and the E Street Band for a hilarious "[Talk to Me]({{< relref "2019-02-26-roll-of-the-dice-talk-to-me.md" >}})" and "Twist and Shout."<br>

**2016:** Bruce opens his River show in Munich with the tour premiere of the Darkness Tour arrangement of "[Prove It All Night]({{< relref "2020-10-24-roll-of-the-dice-prove-it-all-night.md" >}})" and closes with a solo acoustic guitar performance of "[For You]({{< relref "2011-01-07-roll-of-the-dice-for-you.md" >}})." This show is available to stream or purchase [here](https://live.brucespringsteen.net/live-music/0,14115/Bruce-Springsteen-mp3-flac-download-6-17-2016-Olympic-Stadium-Munich-DE.html).<br>

**2020:** Bruce releases Episode 6 ("Down to the River to Pray") of his *From My Home to Yours* radio show on E Street Radio. This episode is a somber one: as the coronavirus pandemic rages and claims lives, Bruce pays his respects to the fallen.<br>

**2023:** On his night off between shows in Birmingham, Bruce attends Steve Earle's solo acoustic show.<br>

**2024:** Bruce and the E Street Band wrap up their three-night stand in Madrid, tour premiering "[Cover Me.]({{< relref "1984-01-04-roll-of-the-dice-cover-me.md" >}})"<br>
