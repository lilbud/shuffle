A fascinating thing happens when a heterosexual woman sings “[Tougher Than the Rest]({{< relref "1987-01-02-roll-of-the-dice-tougher-than-the-rest.md" >}}/).”

When you swap the genders of the singer and the addressee, but you keep the rest of the lyrics intact, the song transforms.

In Bruce’s [*Tunnel of Love*]({{< relref "1987-01-01-album-companion-tunnel-of-love.md" >}}) original, the singer is a self-aware, world-weary man who realizes he isn’t every girl’s dream, but who pledges to be true and constant.

When Tracyanne Campbell sings it, though (or [Shawn Colvin]({{< relref "2018-05-09-cover-me-shawn-colvin-tougher-than-the-rest.md" >}}), or [Tracey Thorn]({{< relref "2018-09-19-cover-me-everything-but-the-girl-tougher-than-the-rest.md" >}}), or [Martina Linn]({{< relref "2018-02-15-cover-me-martina-linn-tougher-than-the-rest.md" >}}), who likewise bring new meaning to the song), she doesn’t *want* every girl’s dream boy. She wants something true, meaningful, and lasting. And just like that, the song changes, but it’s just as powerfully romantic.

{{< youtube 70he4zgJzoU >}}

It’s a beautiful cover by Traceyanne’s band Camera Obscura, and you can find it on the B-side of their 2009 single, “The Sweetest Thing.”
