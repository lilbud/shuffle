***New for 2025:** New entry for 1980 and 1982*<br>

**1968:** The Castiles play a gig at Le Teendezvous in New Shrewsbury, New Jersey.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/04/1968-04-26.jpg.webp)

**1973:** The new issue of *Rolling Stone* features a profile of Bruce, their first of many to come. (Thanks to Andy Swanson for the scan!)<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/04/1973-04-26-scaled.jpg.webp)

**1976:** Bruce and the E Street Band play at the Soldiers & Sailors Memorial Auditorium in Chattanooga, their first of three nights in Tennessee.<br>

**1980:** Bruce and his studio team complete the mixes for "[Cadillac Ranch]({{< relref "2020-07-23-roll-of-the-dice-cadillac-ranch.md" >}})" and "[Two Hearts]({{< relref "2018-05-28-roll-of-the-dice-two-hearts.md" >}})" at Clover Studios in Los Angeles.<br>

{{< youtube BaTNvGQ6tKA >}}

**1981:** Bruce plays his first-ever show in Belgium (and the first ever "Stolen Car" in Europe) when the River Tour arrives in Brussels.<br>

{{< videopress id=UyXUhTpJ >}}

**1982:** Bruce in the studio at the Power Station in New York, recording for what will become *Born in the U.S.A.*<br>

**1994:** "[Streets of Philadelphia]({{< relref "2021-06-30-roll-of-the-dice-streets-of-philadelphia.md" >}})" is certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of America.<br>

{{< youtube h6K0u5Z6JFM >}}

**1998:** Douglas Springsteen, Bruce's father, passes away at the age of 73.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/04/1998-04-26.jpg.webp)

**1999:** Bruce and the E Street Band bring the Reunion Tour to Zurich.<br>

{{< videopress id=5QWqHj9x >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2024/04/Richmond_Times_Dispatch_Wed__Apr_28__1999_.jpg.webp)

**2000:** At Night Two at the Mellon Arena in Pittsburgh, Bruce and the E Street Band perform a rare "[Back in Your Arms]({{< relref "2019-11-10-roll-of-the-dice-back-in-your-arms.md" >}})," and Joe Grushecky guest-stars on "[Hungry Heart]({{< relref "2022-12-21-roll-of-the-dice-hungry-heart.md" >}})."<br>

{{< videopress id=zjtTvBOg >}}

**2005:** Bruce releases *Devils & Dust*, his thirteenth studio album. (The album was released in Europe a day earlier.)<br>

**2006:** Bruce and the Sessions Band play their final rehearsal show at Asbury Park's Convention Hall, opening and closing with debuts of "[Long Black Veil]({{< relref "2020-02-05-cover-me-long-black-veil.md" >}})" and "[The Daring Young Man on the Flying Trapeze]({{< relref "1976-02-01-cover-me-the-daring-young-man-on-the-flying-trapeze.md" >}}/)" respectively.<br>

**2009:** The Working on a Dream Tour stops in Atlanta for a show at the Philips Arena. "[Trapped]({{< relref "2018-08-05-roll-of-the-dice-cover-me-trapped.md" >}})," "[Detroit Medley]({{< relref "1976-01-02-cover-me-detroit-medley.md" >}}/)," and "[96 Tears]({{< relref "2020-02-15-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-garland-jeffreys-96-tears.md" >}})" make their tour debuts.<br>

**2012:** Los Angeles gets a pair of Wrecking Ball shows at the Sports Arena. Night One sees the tour premieres of "[Something in the Night]({{< relref "2021-05-28-roll-of-the-dice-something-in-the-night.md" >}})," "[California Sun]({{< relref "2020-06-10-cover-me-california-sun.md" >}})," and the customary SoCal guest appearance by Tom Morello on "[The Ghost of Tom Joad]({{< relref "2019-10-17-roll-of-the-dice-the-ghost-of-tom-joad.md" >}})." For more highlights from this show, see my [Where the Band Was report]({{< relref "2018-04-26-where-the-band-was-los-angeles-april-26-2012.md" >}}) for this date.<br>
