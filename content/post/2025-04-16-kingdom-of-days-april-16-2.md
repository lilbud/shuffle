***New for 2025:** New entry for 1983 and 2013; video for 2009; review for 2012*<br>

**1971:** Bruce plays two shows at The Upstage in Asbury Park, jamming with (and scouting) local musicians for a new band.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/04/1971-04-16.jpg.webp)

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/04/1971-0416.jpg.webp)

**1976:** Bruce and the E Street Band play Allegheny College in Meadville, Pennsylvania. The school paper pans the concert *twice* by two different reviewers.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/04/1976-0416.jpg.webp)

**1981:** The River Tour continues its European leg with a show in Munich, where "[The Ties That Bind]({{< relref "2018-03-09-roll-of-the-dice-the-ties-that-bind.md" >}})" receives its first European performance. Guest blogger Dan French was at this show; read part two of his epic E Street adventure [here]({{< relref "2021-04-16-where-the-band-was-munich-april-16-1981-dans-story.md" >}}).<br>

{{< videopress id=IahVMWyt >}}

**1982:** Bruce joins Clarence Clemons and the Red Bank Rockers for "[Tenth Avenue Freeze-Out]({{< relref "2018-11-19-roll-of-the-dice-tenth-avenue-freeze-out.md" >}})" at Big Man's West.<br>

**1983:** Bruce works on "[Unsatisfied Heart]({{< relref "1983-01-17-roll-of-the-dice-unsatisfied-heart.md" >}})" at his home studio in Los Angeles.<br>

**1985:** The final night of five in Tokyo on the Born in the U.S.A. Tour.<br>

{{< videopress id=IW12P88W >}}

**1993:** At Night Two in Sheffield, Bruce honors a request and plays a one-off (for the tour) acoustic "[Point Blank]({{< relref "2020-12-19-roll-of-the-dice-point-blank.md" >}})." It's a lovely, unique arrangement and very worth a listen.<br>

{{< videopress id=uFt2pexl >}}

**1996:** Bruce plays the first of two shows at Royal Albert Hall in London and premieres the dubious classic, "[Pilgrim in the Temple of Love]({{< relref "2002-01-12-roll-of-the-dice-pilgrim-in-the-temple-of-love.md" >}}/)." This show was rescheduled from its original scheduled date in March.<br>

{{< videopress id=xlOP1Ixx >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/04/1996-04-16a-1.jpg.webp)

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/04/1996-04-16b-1.jpg.webp)

**2008:** Bruce endorses Senator Barack Obama for president of the United States in an endorsement on his web site. Later, at Sloan-Kettering Hospital in New York City, Bruce and the E Street Band gather at the bedside of ailing bandmate Danny Federici to say their final goodbyes and sing together one last time.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/04/2008-04-16.jpg.webp)

**2009:** At Night Two at the L.A. Sports Arena, Mike Ness guest stars on "[Bad Luck]({{< relref "2020-06-25-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-mike-ness-bad-luck.md" >}})" and "[The Rising]({{< relref "2016-01-18-roll-of-the-dice-the-rising.md" >}})." Making their tour debuts: "[Candy's Room,]({{< relref "2022-05-02-roll-of-the-dice-candys-room.md" >}})" "[Adam Raised a Cain,]({{< relref "2022-07-03-roll-of-the-dice-adam-raised-a-cain.md" >}})" "Proud Mary," "[Hungry Heart]({{< relref "2022-12-21-roll-of-the-dice-hungry-heart.md" >}})," and "[Backstreets]({{< relref "2022-05-14-roll-of-the-dice-backstreets.md" >}})."<br>
