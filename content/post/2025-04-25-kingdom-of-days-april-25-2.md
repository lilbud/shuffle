***New for 2025:** New entry for 1975, 1978, and 1980*<br>

**1973:** Bruce plays two shows at The Main Point in Bryn Mawr, Pennsylvania. (Photo credit: Phil Ceccola)<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/04/1973-0425.jpg.webp)

**1975:** Bruce works on "Jungleland" and "Thunder Road" at the Record Plant in New York City.<br>

**1978:** Bruce and his studio team complete the mix for "[Prove It All Night]({{< relref "2020-10-24-roll-of-the-dice-prove-it-all-night.md" >}})" at the Record Plant in New York City.<br>

{{< youtube UzjkWdRqWFo >}}

**1980:** Bruce and his studio team complete the mix for "[Independence Day]({{< relref "2022-03-14-roll-of-the-dice-independence-day.md" >}})" at Clover studios in Los Angeles.<br>

{{< youtube 9GggK3Sn258 >}}

**1982:** Bruce catches Cats on a Smooth Surface performing at The Stone Pony in Asbury Park, but he does not take the stage.<br>

**1988:** Night Three (of five) for the Tunnel of Love Express Tour at the L.A. Sports Arena.<br>

{{< youtube yvQNGDE0pFY >}}

{{< videopress id=77lAhHBZ >}}

**1989:** At Micky Rourke's Rubber Club in Los Angeles, Bruce joins a local band on stage for "[Stand By Me]({{< relref "2021-12-12-matr-u2-and-bruce-springsteen-stand-by-me.md" >}})."<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/04/1989-04-25.jpg.webp)

**1996:** Elliott Murphy and Joe Grushecky join Bruce for a final encore of "[Blowin' Down This Road]({{< relref "2020-05-27-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-friends-blowin-down-this-road.md" >}})" at Bruce's second solo acoustic show at Brixton Academy in London.<br>

{{< youtube 3vI9BTN8l8E >}}

{{< videopress id=4cjlbQWl >}}

**2000:** The first of two Reunion shows at Mellon Arena in Pittsburgh features the first E Street Band performance of "[Human Touch]({{< relref "2019-08-09-roll-of-the-dice-human-touch.md" >}})" and a Joe Grushecky guest appearance on "[Idiot's Delight]({{< relref "2021-03-12-rotd-matr-joe-grushecky-and-bruce-springsteen-idiots-delight.md" >}})."<br>

{{< videopress id=txuqoZGk >}}

{{< videopress id=ZCVQXnYW >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/04/2000-04-25a.jpg.webp)

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/04/2000-04-25b.jpg.webp)

**2004:** Bruce plays a benefit for Rumson Country Day School (along with Patti, Southside Johnny, and Bobby Bandiera) for the third year running at The Stone Pony in Asbury Park.<br>

**2005:** Bruce kicks off his solo acoustic tour with a show at the Fox Theater in Detroit.<br>

**2006:** Bruce releases [*We Shall Overcome: The Seeger Sessions*]({{< relref "2006-01-01-album-companion-we-shall-overcome.md" >}})and appears on *Good Morning America* to promote the album and upcoming tour.<br>

Turn! Turn (To Everything There is a Season)]({{< relref "2021-06-09-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-roger-mcguinn-turn-turn-turn.md" >}})."

**2008:** The Magic Tour moves to Atlanta for a show at Philips Arena, where "[Blinded by the Light]({{< relref "2011-01-02-roll-of-the-dice-blinded-by-the-light.md" >}})" makes its tour premiere.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/04/2008-04-25b.jpg.webp)

**2016:** The closing night of the first U.S. leg of the River Tour (2016 version) at Barclays Center in Brooklyn brings the tour premieres of "[Boom Boom]({{< relref "2020-03-26-cover-me-boom-boom.md" >}})" and "[Trapped]({{< relref "2018-08-05-roll-of-the-dice-cover-me-trapped.md" >}})" along with a reprise of Bruce's "[Purple Rain]({{< relref "2021-03-03-cover-me-purple-rain.md" >}})" tribute, a rare "[Loose Ends,]({{< relref "2022-06-23-roll-of-the-dice-loose-end.md" >}})" and an adorable fan singalong to "[Blinded by the Light]({{< relref "2011-01-02-roll-of-the-dice-blinded-by-the-light.md" >}})."<br>
