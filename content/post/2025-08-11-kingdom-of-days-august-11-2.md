***New for 2025:** New entry for 1977; audio for 2003*<br>

**1972:** Bruce plays his third of six two-shows-a-night solo gigs at Max's Kansas City that week.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2019/08/1972-08-11.jpg.webp)

**1974:** Bruce cancels a gig at the Cambridge Performance Center to make room for *Born to Run* recording sessions.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2019/08/1974-08-11.jpg.webp)

**1977:** Bruce holds a recording session for his upcoming album at Atlantic Studios in New York City.<br>

**1981:** Bruce and the E Street Band bring the River Tour to Detroit's Joe Louis Arena for the first of two nights.<br>

{{< videopress id=j4p8FdMJ >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/07/1981-08-11.png.webp)

**1984:** At Night Five (of ten) at the Brendan Byrne Arena in East Rutherford, John Entwistle guest-stars on the show-closing "Twist and Shout."<br>

{{< videopress id=D7LMS4B8 >}}

**1985:** At Pittsburgh's Three Rivers Stadium, Nils and Roy miss their signal to take the stage, so Bruce starts the show and plays "[Born in the U.S.A.]({{< relref "1984-01-03-roll-of-the-dice-born-in-the-u-s-a.md" >}})" without them. Bruce will do a pre-show headcount ever after.<br>

{{< videopress id=LBLlimMh >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/08/1985-08-11a.jpg.webp)

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/08/1985-08-11b-scaled.jpg.webp)

**1989:** Bruce makes a surprise guest appearance at the Garden State Arts Center in Holmdel, joining Ringo Starr and His All Starr Band (including Nils and Clarence) for the encores of "[Get Back]({{< relref "2020-07-22-matr-ringo-starr-and-bruce-springsteen-get-back.md" >}})," "[Long Tall Sally]({{< relref "2019-04-19-cover-me-long-tall-sally.md" >}})," "[Photograph]({{< relref "2018-04-29-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-ringo-starr-and-his-all-starr-band-photograph.md" >}})" and "[With a Little Help From My Friends]({{< relref "2018-06-03-matr-ringo-starrs-all-starr-band-bruce-springsteen-and-john-candy-with-a-little-help-from-my-friends.md" >}})."<br>

**1999:** It's the penultimate show of Bruce and the E Street Band's fifteen-night Reunion Tour stand at East Rutherford's Continental Airlines Arena, and the show rockets out of the gate with the first performance of "[Night]({{< relref "2019-10-31-roll-of-the-dice-night.md" >}})" in 15 years. Later in the show: the first "New York City Serenade" in 24 years, and the show-closing tour premiere of "[4th of July, Asbury Park (Sandy)]({{< relref "2019-04-26-roll-of-the-dice-4th-of-july-asbury-park-sandy.md" >}})."<br>

**2003:** Bruce and the E Street Band wrap up their three-show Rising stand at Philadelphia's Lincoln Financial Field in peak form, opening with "From Small Things (Big Things One Day Come)," and tour premiering "[I'm Goin' Down]({{< relref "1984-01-11-roll-of-the-dice-im-goin-down.md" >}})" and "[Pretty Flamingo]({{< relref "2021-12-04-cover-me-pretty-flamingo.md" >}})" (the latter of which hadn't been played in 25 years). Throw in a rare "[Incident on 57th Street]({{< relref "2018-12-14-roll-of-the-dice-incident-on-57th-street.md" >}})" "[Be True]({{< relref "2020-12-02-roll-of-the-dice-be-true.md" >}})," and a show-closing "[I'm a Rocker]({{< relref "2020-09-18-roll-of-the-dice-im-a-rocker.md" >}})," and you've got the makings of a killer setlist. And then there's that "[Ramrod]({{< relref "2022-09-10-roll-of-the-dice-ramrod.md" >}})" under a full moon...<br>

**2005:** Bruce continues his solo acoustic swing through the Pacific Northwest with a show at Seattle's Key Arena, where Bruce tour premieres "[Be True]({{< relref "2020-12-02-roll-of-the-dice-be-true.md" >}})" and plays a one-off guitar version of "[For You]({{< relref "2011-01-07-roll-of-the-dice-for-you.md" >}})." For more highlights from this show, see my [*Where the Band Was* report]({{< relref "2018-08-11-where-the-band-was-seattle-august-11-2005.md" >}}).<br>

**2023:** Bruce and the E Street Band play their second of two shows at Wrigley Field in Chicago.<br>
