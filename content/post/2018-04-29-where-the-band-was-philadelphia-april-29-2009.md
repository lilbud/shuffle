It's a rule that Night Two of a two-night stand is always better than [Night One]({{< relref "2018-04-28-where-the-band-was-philadelphia-april-28-2009.md" >}}). Bruce tends to play it safe on the opening night and let loose on the last night.

But in Philly, *all* nights are special, and at these first two of six shows on the Working on a Dream Tour (the other four were six months away), it's a toss-up as to which was the stronger of the two.

I'll set "[The Fever]({{< relref "2020-10-04-roll-of-the-dice-the-fever.md" >}})" aside, because nothing would top the exhilaration I felt hearing that for the first time. The only thing that would come close is hearing "[Kitty's Back,]({{< relref "2022-01-22-roll-of-the-dice-kittys-back.md" >}})" which moved to the number one spot on my wishlist when I retired "The Fever" 24 hours prior.

What did Bruce deal Philly in the wild card pack on this night? First up, the E Street Band premiere of The Clash's "[London Calling,]({{< relref "2018-04-09-matr-bruce-springsteen-elvis-costello-steve-van-zandt-dave-grohl-london-calling.md" >}})" played by Bruce only once prior, at the Grammy Awards six years earlier; next, a rollicking arrangement of "[Red Headed Woman]({{< relref "2021-09-15-roll-of-the-dice-red-headed-woman.md" >}})" to welcome Patti back to the stage (she'd missed a few shows due to an injury), and finally the best performance of "[Thundercrack]({{< relref "2021-09-24-roll-of-the-dice-thundercrack.md" >}})" I've had the privilege to see (somehow, I've managed to catch this rarity five times).

Next up: "[Waitin' on a Sunny Day]({{< relref "2019-09-27-roll-of-the-dice-waitin-on-a-sunny-day.md" >}})" gets a night off, with "[Hungry Heart]({{< relref "2022-12-21-roll-of-the-dice-hungry-heart.md" >}})" a welcome substitute. Bruce's mom even contributed some guest vocals:

"[The Wrestler]({{< relref "2020-10-31-roll-of-the-dice-the-wrestler.md" >}})" also took a break–Bruce swapped it for "[Streets of Philadelphia]({{< relref "2021-06-30-roll-of-the-dice-streets-of-philadelphia.md" >}})" instead–another song on my wish list.

{{< youtube JLjI7cUGpvw >}}

Then came the encores, and once again, Bruce pitched Philly a bonus: "Thunder Road," dedicated to the late Harry Kalas, whose customer voiceover for Bruce sent the crowd roaring.

{{< youtube 5aGxv2Y7Mto >}}

…but Bruce *still* wasn't done. After "[American Land]({{< relref "2016-01-22-roll-of-the-dice-american-land.md" >}})," I waited to see which final number Bruce would send us home with. It had been "Rosalita (Come Out Tonight)" the night before, so it was almost certain to be "[Glory Days]({{< relref "1984-01-12-roll-of-the-dice-glory-days.md" >}})" tonight. (He'd been alternating between them for most of the tour.) Or maybe it would be "[Dancing in the Dark]({{< relref "1984-01-13-roll-of-the-dice-dancing-in-the-dark.md" >}})"–that chestnut had made a few appearances in that slot, too.

But it wasn't any of them. On this night, Bruce pulled out a tour debut–and unbelievably, I got my #1 most-chased song only 24 hours after getting my previous one: Bruce sent us off into the night dancing to the strains of an epic 13-minute "Kitty's Back." It didn't seem like Bruce ever wanted to stop–there was even a reprise–and those last three minutes were among the most joyous I've ever experienced at a Springsteen show.
