***New for 2025:** New entry for 1973, 1977 and 1979; video for 2023*<br>

**1971:** The Bruce Springsteen Band opens for Humble Pie at Asbury Park's Sunshine In. Peter Frampton is impressed enough by Bruce and the band to offer them a gig opening for Humble Pie around the country, as well as an introduction to their record label. Mystifyingly, Tinker West (Bruce's manager at the time) declines the offers.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2019/06/1971-07-11.jpg.webp)

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/06/1971-07-11.jpg.webp)

**1973:** Bruce records overdubs for "[Kitty's Back]({{< relref "2022-01-22-roll-of-the-dice-kittys-back.md" >}})" and "[Evacuation of the West]({{< relref "2020-09-16-roll-of-the-dice-evacuation-of-the-west.md" >}})" at 914 Sound Studios in Blauvelt, New York.<br>

**1977:** Bruce and the E Street Band work on "[Badlands]({{< relref "2016-01-20-roll-of-the-dice-badlands.md" >}})" at Atlantic Studio in New York City.<br>

**1979:** Bruce and the E Street Band work on "[I Wanna Marry You]({{< relref "2022-04-22-roll-of-the-dice-i-wanna-marry-you.md" >}})" during a recording session at the Power Station in New York City.<br>

**1981:** Clarence Clemons opens his new nightclub, Big Man's West, in Red Bank. Bruce makes an appearance with Clarence and his band to mark the occasion, playing six songs, including a set closing "Y[ou Can't Sit Down](../2018-06-21-cover-me-you-cant-sit-down/post.md/)" and "[Cadillac Ranch]({{< relref "2020-07-23-roll-of-the-dice-cadillac-ranch.md" >}})."<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/06/1981-07-11-rotated.jpg.webp)

**2002:** Bruce kicks off rehearsals for the Rising Tour with the E Street Band at the Expo Theater in Fort Monmouth, New Jersey.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2019/06/2002-07-11.jpg.webp)

**2008:** It's Christmas in July when Bruce honors one last sign request when the Magic Tour stops in Helsinki: "[Santa Claus is Comin' to Town]({{< relref "2021-12-24-cover-me-santa-claus-is-comin-to-town.md" >}})" is the second to last song of the night.<br>

{{< youtube 0stcLuNJlIQ >}}

**2009:** Jake Clemons makes his debut on an E Street Band stage when he guest-stars on sax during "[Tenth Avenue Freeze-Out]({{< relref "2018-11-19-roll-of-the-dice-tenth-avenue-freeze-out.md" >}})" as the Working on a Dream Tour stops for the first of two nights at RDS Arena in Dublin. (How strange to see him next to Clarence–the intersection of E Street Past and Present!) Bruce's son Evan joins the band as well, playing acoustic guitar on "[American Land]({{< relref "2016-01-22-roll-of-the-dice-american-land.md" >}})." Tour debuting tonight: "[You Can Look (But You Better Not Touch)]({{< relref "2020-06-14-roll-of-the-dice-you-can-look-but-you-better-not-touch.md" >}})."<br>

**2016:** A wild night in Paris for the first of two shows on the River Tour (2016 Edition). Bruce opens with a solo piano "[Incident on 57th Street]({{< relref "2018-12-14-roll-of-the-dice-incident-on-57th-street.md" >}})" followed by the tour premiere of "[Reason to Believe]({{< relref "2021-02-27-roll-of-the-dice-reason-to-believe.md" >}})." "Into the Fire" and "[Nebraska]({{< relref "2018-04-16-roll-of-the-dice-nebraska.md" >}})" receive tour premieres as well. Elliott and Gaspard Murphy make a customary guest appearance during the encores, but during "[Ramrod]({{< relref "2022-09-10-roll-of-the-dice-ramrod.md" >}})," the power goes out and Bruce finishes the song mic-less before taking a break until the power is restored. This show is available to stream or purchase [here](https://live.brucespringsteen.net/live-music/0,14124/Bruce-Springsteen-mp3-flac-download-7-11-2016-AccorHotels-Arena-Paris-FR.html).<br>
