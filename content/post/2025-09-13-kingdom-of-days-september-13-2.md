**1968:** Earth plays two shows at the Off Broad Street Coffee House in Red Bank, New Jersey.<br>

**1984:** Washington Post columnist George Will publishes a column praising Bruce as a paragon of conservative American values, sparking a debate over the meaning behind Bruce's music and performance. Within a week, the president of the United States will weigh in.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/09/1984-09-13.jpg.webp)

**1985:** At the MTV Video Music Awards, "[I'm on Fire]({{< relref "1984-01-08-roll-of-the-dice-im-on-fire.md" >}})" wins the award for Best Male Video, and "[Dancing in the Dark]({{< relref "1984-01-13-roll-of-the-dice-dancing-in-the-dark.md" >}})" wins the award for Best Stage Performance in a Video (but loses to Philip Bailey and Phil Collins' "Easy Lover" in the Best Overall Performance in a Video category). Little Steven and John Sayles are on hand to accept the award on Bruce's behalf (ironically, since Little Steven is no longer in the E Street Band).<br>

{{< videopress id=12KMj7U1 >}}

**2003:** The Rising Tour is in its last month, and the set lists are getting looser. Bruce opens his show at FedEx Field in Landover, Maryland, with his first ever performance of "[I Walk the Line]({{< relref "2020-06-16-cover-me-i-walk-the-line.md" >}})" in memory of the recently departed Johnny Cash. Bruce also tour premieres "[Pink Cadillac]({{< relref "2019-08-21-roll-of-the-dice-pink-cadillac.md" >}})" (played for the first time in 18 years) and "[Paradise]({{< relref "2018-01-07-roll-of-the-dice-paradise.md" >}})" (not played since the rehearsal shows over a year earlier).<br>
