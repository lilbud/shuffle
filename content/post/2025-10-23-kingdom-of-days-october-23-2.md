**1970:** Steel Mill headlines a show at the University of Richmond.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/10/1970-1023.jpg.webp)

**1975:** Bruce meets Bob Dylan for the first time at a birthday party for Mike Porco, owner of Gerde's Folk City in New York City. Bruce and Dylan join others present to sing "Happy Birthday" to Porco, making this (sort of) their first performance together. The event is filmed, but no Bruce footage will be released. Reportedly, Dylan invites Bruce to join his Rolling Thunder Revue as a solo performer, but Bruce declines.<br>

**1980:** Montana musician Phil Hamilton–a devoted Bruce fan–arranges a two-night stand in Seattle for his Lost Highway Band in order to be in town for Bruce's River show the next day. Coincidentally, Hamilton spots Bruce walking on the street, introduces himself, and invites Bruce to come watch their show at The Old Timer's Cafe that night. Bruce shocks the band by doing just that, even joining them on stage for a few songs.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/10/1980-1023.jpg.webp)

**1987:** Bruce performs "[Forever Young]({{< relref "2019-09-01-matr-bob-dylan-and-bruce-springsteen-forever-young.md" >}})" acoustically at the funeral of John Hammond at St. Peter's Church in New York City.<br>

**1996:** Bruce plays a solo acoustic performance at the William Saroyan Theater in Fresno, where he plays "[The New Timer]({{< relref "2018-07-13-roll-of-the-dice-the-new-timer.md" >}})" for the last time for nine years.<br>

**2012:** Bruce performs solo at a re-election rally for President Obama in Charlottesville, Virginia. That night, he and the E Street Band play a Wrecking Ball show at the John Paul Jones Arena, where the fantastic set includes the tour premiere of "[Give the Girl a Kiss]({{< relref "2020-06-17-roll-of-the-dice-give-the-girl-a-kiss.md" >}})," a rare "[Seaside Bar Song]({{< relref "2022-10-24-roll-of-the-dice-seaside-bar-song.md" >}})" and "[This Depression]({{< relref "2020-02-21-roll-of-the-dice-this-depression.md" >}})," and three epics: "[Lost in the Flood]({{< relref "2011-01-05-roll-of-the-dice-lost-in-the-flood.md" >}}/)," "[Backstreets]({{< relref "2022-05-14-roll-of-the-dice-backstreets.md" >}})" and "Jungleland." Friend of the blog Bill Kenney is there and shares his first-hand review [here](https://billk128.tumblr.com/post/35088354431/live-from-the-hook-or-prelude-to-sandy).<br>
