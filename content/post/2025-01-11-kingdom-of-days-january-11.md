**1942:** Clarence “Big Man” Clemons is born in Norfolk, Virginia.<br>

**1973:** Bruce and the band continue their Boston residency with two shows at Paul’s Mall.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2019/12/1973-01-11.jpg.webp)

**1979:** Bruce and the E Street Band play a set of cover tunes at a private 37th birthday party for Clarence at Lock Stock & Barrel in Fair Haven, New Jersey.<br>

**1980:** At his home studio in Holmdel, Bruce and the E Street Band rehearse material for their upcoming recording sessions for *The River.*<br>

**1996:** Bruce plays the second of two shows at Detroit’s Fox Theater.<br>

**2002:** On the occasion of Clarence’s 60th birthday, Bruce joins Clarence on stage at B.B. King’s nightclub in Mashantucket, Connecticut. They play four songs together, including “[Glory Days]({{< relref "1984-01-12-roll-of-the-dice-glory-days.md" >}})” with B.B. King.<br>

**2009**: At the 66th Annual Golden Globes, Bruce wins the award for Best Original Song for “[The Wrestler]({{< relref "2020-10-31-roll-of-the-dice-the-wrestler.md" >}})” (and wishes Clarence a happy birthday in his acceptance speech).<br>
