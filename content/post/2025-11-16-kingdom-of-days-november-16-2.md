**1990:** Bruce plays the first of a pair of unique, remarkable benefit shows for the Christic Institute, a public interest law firm. This is Bruce's first "official" concert since disbanding the E Street Band. He's out of his comfort zone, and it shows. His life has changed: he's firmly attached to Patti now (even if they're not yet married), he's a father, and his new life perspective informs the new work that comprises much of the setlist. World premieres abound: "[Red Headed Woman]({{< relref "2021-09-15-roll-of-the-dice-red-headed-woman.md" >}})," "[57 Channels (And Nothin' On)]({{< relref "2020-05-17-roll-of-the-dice-57-channels-and-nothin-on.md" >}})," "[When the Lights Go Out]({{< relref "2021-01-23-roll-of-the-dice-when-the-lights-go-out.md" >}})," and "[Real World]({{< relref "2022-07-25-roll-of-the-dice-real-world.md" >}})" all get their very first airings. Intimate performances of "[My Father's House]({{< relref "2021-08-13-roll-of-the-dice-my-fathers-house.md" >}})," "[Brilliant Disguise]({{< relref "1987-01-09-roll-of-the-dice-brilliant-disguise.md" >}}/)" and "[Tenth Avenue Freeze-Out]({{< relref "2018-11-19-roll-of-the-dice-tenth-avenue-freeze-out.md" >}})" (which sounds particularly forlorn tonight) take on new life. In the encores, Bonnie Raitt and Jackson Browne join Bruce for "[Highway 61 Revisite](../2018-11-07-meeting-across-the-river-bruce-springsteen-bob-dylan-and-friends-highway-61-revisited/post.md/)d" and a gorgeous rendition of "[Across the Borderline]({{< relref "2022-03-26-matr-bruce-springsteen-bonnie-raitt-and-jackson-browne-across-the-borderline.md" >}})." This show is available for purchase as an official archive series installment (get it [here](https://live.brucespringsteen.net/live-music/0,14250/Bruce-Springsteen-mp3-flac-download-11-16-1990-The-Christic-Shows-at-The-Shrine-Los-Angeles-CA.html)) and is essential for any Springsteen fan.<br>

**2002:** Bruce tour premieres "[Cadillac Ranch]({{< relref "2020-07-23-roll-of-the-dice-cadillac-ranch.md" >}})" and plays a rare solo piano "[Incident on 57th Street]({{< relref "2018-12-14-roll-of-the-dice-incident-on-57th-street.md" >}})" at his Rising show in Greensboro, but it's his fall during "[Waitin' on a Sunny Day]({{< relref "2019-09-27-roll-of-the-dice-waitin-on-a-sunny-day.md" >}})" that almost ends up a literal show-stopper. Bruce stopped a serious fall by a deft save using Max's drum stand, but the mishap was enough to draw blood, shocking fans who watched Bruce just continue on as if nothing had happened.<br>

**2010:** Bruce releases *The Promise: The Darkness on the Edge of Town Story* box set, featuring a new double-album of previously unreleased songs. That evening, he makes a guest appearance on *Late Night with Jimmy Fallon*, masquerading as "1975 Bruce" to perform "[Whip My Hair]({{< relref "2019-09-05-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-jimmy-fallon-whip-my-hair.md" >}})" with Fallon (who is masquerading as Neil Young). Once rid of the disguise, Bruce performs "Because the Night" (with Patti Smith's lyrics) and "[Save My Love]({{< relref "2019-07-16-roll-of-the-dice-save-my-love.md" >}})," accompanied by Steve, Roy, and The Roots.<br>
