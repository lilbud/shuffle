***New for 2025:** New entry for 1983 and 2017*<br>

**1976:** Bruce and the E Street Band play for the U.S. Military Academy at West Point.<br>

{{< videopress id=10bHWUP3 >}}

**1978:** At Night Two in Philadelphia on the Darkness Tour, the core of the set finally gels, as fans hear the first consecutive plays of "[The Promised Land]({{< relref "2016-01-09-roll-of-the-dice-the-promised-land.md" >}})," "[Prove It All Night]({{< relref "2020-10-24-roll-of-the-dice-prove-it-all-night.md" >}})," "[Racing in the Street]({{< relref "2021-10-03-roll-of-the-dice-racing-in-the-street.md" >}})," "Thunder Road," and "Jungleland." This core five-pack will last throughout the rest of the tour.<br>

{{< videopress id=LvrfSAAA >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/05/1978-05-26.jpg.webp)

**1979:** Bruce joins Robert Gordon on stage for "[Fire]({{< relref "2020-08-05-roll-of-the-dice-fire.md" >}})" at the Paramount Theater in Asbury Park. Garry joins them for "[Heartbreak Hotel]({{< relref "2021-04-11-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-robert-gordon-heartbreak-hotel.md" >}})" as well.<br>

{{< youtube kdSQPILL2Nw >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/05/1979-05-27.jpg.webp)

**1981:** At the Brighton Center on the River Tour, Bruce and the E Street Band perform "[The Price You Pay]({{< relref "2019-09-23-roll-of-the-dice-the-price-you-pay.md" >}})" for the last time for 28 years.<br>

{{< videopress id=sOEKNAFK >}}

**1983:** Bruce and the E Street Band record at The Hit Factory in New York City for *Born in the U.S.A.*, although none of the tracks from today's session will make the album.<br>

**1985:** Bruce and the E Street Band (plus Little Steven, director John Sayles and crew) take over Maxwell's in Hoboken for the first of two days of filming for the video for "[Glory Days.]({{< relref "1984-01-12-roll-of-the-dice-glory-days.md" >}})" Watch local news coverage below.<br>

{{< youtube NeJrkyzmyS0 >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/05/1985-05-27.jpg.webp)

**1999:** Bruce and the E Street Band make a Reunion Tour stop at the Flanders Expo in Ghent, where Elliott Murphy guest-stars on "[Hungry Heart]({{< relref "2022-12-21-roll-of-the-dice-hungry-heart.md" >}})" and "Born to Run."<br>

{{< videopress id=n9SXCX1I >}}

**2000:** Bruce plays his first-ever show in Las Vegas when the Reunion Tour stops at the MGM Grand Garden Arena. Bruce opens and closes with the premiere of "[Viva Las Vegas]({{< relref "2018-12-07-cover-me-viva-las-vegas.md" >}})," and he tour premieres "[Can't Help Falling in Love]({{< relref "1976-01-21-cover-me-cant-help-falling-in-love.md" >}}/)" for the occasion as well.<br>

{{< youtube-playlist PLJTyL9twk3zC6-aOkAImq5An0nRfHsd63 >}}

**2001:** Bruce and Graham Parker join Southside Johnny and the Asbury Jukes at The Stone Pony's 25th anniversary celebration of Southside's live radio broadcast.<br>
