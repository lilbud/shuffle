***New for 2025:** Audio for 2005 and 2014; video for 2014*<br>

**1981:** Bruce and the E Street Band play their second River Tour show in Edinburgh, Scotland, with "[The Ties That Bind]({{< relref "2018-03-09-roll-of-the-dice-the-ties-that-bind.md" >}})" in the opening spot for the first time.<br>

**1993:** Bruce wraps up the German leg of his World Tour with a show in Mannheim, closing with the tour premiere of "[It's All Right](../1976-01-25-cover-me-its-all-right/post.md/)."<br>

**2005:** Bruce shocks fans at the Tower Theater in Philadelphia when he performs "[Iceman]({{< relref "2022-02-01-roll-of-the-dice-iceman.md" >}})" for the very first time, on piano. "[Ramrod]({{< relref "2022-09-10-roll-of-the-dice-ramrod.md" >}})" makes its tour premiere, and during the soundcheck, Bruce covers Dominic Behan's "[The Patriot Game]({{< relref "2021-04-08-cover-me-the-patriot-game.md" >}})" on pump organ. This show is available as part of Bruce's official archive series–get it [here](https://live.brucespringsteen.net/live-music/0,27134/Bruce-Springsteen-mp3-flac-download-5-17-2005-Tower-Theater-Upper-Darby-PA.html).<br>

**2012:** An amazing Wrecking Ball show in Barcelona, where the tour premieres ("[Hungry Heart]({{< relref "2022-12-21-roll-of-the-dice-hungry-heart.md" >}})" and "Y[ou Can Look (But You Better Not Touch)]({{< relref "2020-06-14-roll-of-the-dice-you-can-look-but-you-better-not-touch.md" >}})" are the least of the highlights. This show will be remembered as the night when the legendary extended instrumental intro to "[Prove It All Night]({{< relref "2020-10-24-roll-of-the-dice-prove-it-all-night.md" >}})" from the Darkness Tour finally returned–by request–after being absent for 32 years.<br>

**2014:** The first night of a jaw-dropping two-night closing stand for the High Hopes Tour in an unusual venue: the Mohegan Sun Casino in Uncasville, Connecticut. The set list features an embarrassment of riches from the opening "[Racing in the Street '78]({{< relref "2021-10-03-roll-of-the-dice-racing-in-the-street.md" >}})" to the tour premiere of "[The Price You Pay]({{< relref "2019-09-23-roll-of-the-dice-the-price-you-pay.md" >}})" and "[Quarter to Three](../2021-08-21-cover-me-quarter-to-three/post.md/)" to the closing "Thunder Road." Little Steven makes his unannounced and triumphant return to the band for these last two shows. For many more highlights from this incredible show, see my [*Where the Band Was* report]({{< relref "2018-05-17-where-the-band-was-uncasville-may-17-2014.md" >}}) from this night.<br>

**2016:** *The River* tracks still dominate the set list as the European leg gathers steam, but there's still plenty of room for tour premieres: "[Fire]({{< relref "2020-08-05-roll-of-the-dice-fire.md" >}})," "[Murder Incorporated]({{< relref "1984-01-15-roll-of-the-dice-murder-incorporated.md" >}})," "[Waitin' on a Sunny Day]({{< relref "2019-09-27-roll-of-the-dice-waitin-on-a-sunny-day.md" >}})" and a solo acoustic "This Hard Land" all make their debut in San Sebastian.<br>
