***New for 2025:** New entry for 1980; audio for 1985 and 2008; video for 2009*<br>

**1966:** The Castiles perform and compete at a Battle of the Bands at the Matawan-Keyport Roller Drome in Matawan, New Jersey. The Castiles do not win (or even place in the top five), but Bruce meets the drummer of the second place band, Vini Lopez, and one of the judges, Vinnie Roslin, for the first time. Vini, Vinnie and Bruce (along with Danny Federici) will go on to form Steel Mill.<br>

**1996:** Thanks to the request of a fan who snuck his way into the soundcheck, Bruce plays "[The Angel]({{< relref "2011-01-06-roll-of-the-dice-the-angel.md" >}})" for the first time ever (and the last time for 13 years) at his solo acoustic show in Royal Albert Hall in London.<br>

**2005:** At a second public rehearsal/benefit show (held in the afternoon) at Asbury Park's Paramount Theater for his upcoming solo tour, Bruce premieres "[Reno]({{< relref "2020-05-21-roll-of-the-dice-reno.md" >}})" and surprises fans with the return of the Christic arrangement of "[Real World]({{< relref "2022-07-25-roll-of-the-dice-real-world.md" >}})." The bullet mic makes its debut on "[Reason to Believe]({{< relref "2021-02-27-roll-of-the-dice-reason-to-believe.md" >}})" and will remain a fixture on both this tour as well as the Magic Tour (albeit with very different arrangements).<br>

**2008:** Bruce and the E Street Band play their first show since the passing of Danny Federici. The Tampa show opens with a tribute film set to "[Blood Brothers]({{< relref "2019-12-06-roll-of-the-dice-blood-brothers.md" >}})" and opens with an emotional "[Backstreets]({{< relref "2022-05-14-roll-of-the-dice-backstreets.md" >}})." The band kicks of the encores with a gorgeous rendition of the gospel classic "[I'll Fly Away]({{< relref "2018-10-16-cover-me-ill-fly-away.md" >}})." This show is available for purchase as part of Bruce's official archive series–get it [here](https://live.brucespringsteen.net/live-music/0,21216/Bruce-Springsteen---The-E-Street-Band-mp3-flac-download-4-22-2008-St-Pete-Times-Forum-Tampa-FL.html).<br>
