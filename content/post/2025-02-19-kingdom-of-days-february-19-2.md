**1971:** Bruce jams with local musicians at an open mic night at the Upstage.<br>

**1972:** The Bruce Springsteen Band plays their fifth of a nine-show, month-long semi-residency at The Back Door in Richmond.<br>

**1975:** Bruce and the E Street Band headline at Penn State University, their first-ever appearance there.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/02/1975-0219.jpg.webp)

**1977:** Bruce brings the Lawsuit Tour to St. Paul, where a fan films parts of the performance on 8mm film.<br>

Also on this date in 1977: Manfred Mann’s Earth Band gifts Bruce with his only number one single. Their cover of “[Blinded by the Light]({{< relref "2011-01-02-roll-of-the-dice-blinded-by-the-light.md" >}})” tops the charts in the U.S. and Canada today.

{{< youtube 33ptulhhQPg >}}

**1984:** Nils Lofgren spends the weekend with Bruce at his house in Rumson. Not long after, Bruce will invite Nils to join the E Street Band.<br>

**2003:** Bruce plays a solo acoustic show in Somerville, Massachusetts, as a benefit for *Doubletake* *Magazine*. This show features the premiere of “[The Wall]({{< relref "2024-09-04-roll-of-the-dice-the-wall.md" >}})” (which won’t be released for more than a decade) and a one-off acoustic “[Souls of the Departed]({{< relref "1992-02-10-roll-of-the-dice-souls-of-the-departed.md" >}}).” The real highlight, though, is the post-show Q&A sessions with fans in attendance. **2014:** Bruce and the E Street Band play the Allphones Arena in Sydney, Australia, where they open with a cover of “[Friday on My Mind]({{< relref "2019-06-07-cover-me-friday-on-my-mind.md" >}})” by The Easybeats. Another surprise cover kicks off the encores: “[Don’t Change]({{< relref "2021-03-26-cover-me-dont-change.md" >}})” by INXS, followed by a blazingly fast “Born to Run.” Also tour premiering tonight: “[Racing in the Street,]({{< relref "2021-10-03-roll-of-the-dice-racing-in-the-street.md" >}})” “[Surprise, Surprise]({{< relref "2021-05-04-roll-of-the-dice-surprise-surprise.md" >}}),” and “[Dream Baby Dream]({{< relref "2020-04-02-cover-me-dream-baby-dream.md" >}}).”<br>
