***New for 2025:** No updates*<br>

**1974:** Bruce stops by KLOL-FM in Houston in the afternoon for an interview with DJ Ed Beauchamp.<br>

{{< youtube kzXpb_tv7-0 >}}

That evening, Bruce and the (still unnamed) E Street Band play two shows at Liberty Hall.

**1975:** The first of two nights at DAR Constitution Hall in Washington, DC.<br>

{{< videopress id=4B0zuMOh >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/03/1975-0308.jpg.webp)

**1982:** In a Los Angeles recording studio, Bruce gives his song "[Protection]({{< relref "2021-12-26-rotd-cover-me-donna-summer-protection.md" >}})" to Donna Summer and provides backing vocals and guitar on the track, while Roy plays piano.<br>

**1999:** Bruce and the E Street Band rehearse for their upcoming Reunion Tour at Asbury Park's Convention Hall.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/03/1999-03-08.jpg.webp)

**2008:** Bruce drops by a benefit for the Ranney School at the Eagle Oaks Country Club in Farmingdale, New Jersey and plays "[Glory Days]({{< relref "1984-01-12-roll-of-the-dice-glory-days.md" >}})" with Tim McLoone and the Shirleys. (photo credit: backstreets.com)<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/03/2008-0308.jpg.webp)

**2018:** *Springsteen on Broadway* continues its theatrical run at the Walter Kerr Theater in New York City.<br>

**2021:** Bruce and President Obama release the fourth episode of their podcast series, *Renegades: Born in the U.S.A.* This episode is entitled "Born to Run: The Loss of Innocence," in which Bruce and Barack talk about travelling across America and finding home.<br>

[Spotify Embed: Traveling the U.S. & Finding Home](https://open.spotify.com/embed/episode/25YjJeERNhvygT4kXBzY4o?si=80ef3d4930854203&utm_source=oembed)

**2022:** At the Fire Department Building in his hometown of Freehold, Bruce appears at a community event announcing the development of *My Hometown: The Bruce Springsteen Story Center*, slated to open in that space in 2024.<br>
