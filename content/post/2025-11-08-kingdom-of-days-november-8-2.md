**1996:** Bruce plays a very special acoustic show at a very special venue: his old elementary school, St. Rose of Lima. This isn't his first time playing the school gym (that was back in his Castiles days), but it's his first time back since then. Although this show is technically part of the Ghost of Tom Joad Tour, in reality it's a very different performance. First of all, he isn't solo: Patti and Soozie join him on several numbers. Secondly, the setlist is *very* different, with *nine* tour premieres. Bruce opens with a gorgeous, Soozie-accompanied "[The River]({{< relref "2021-03-04-roll-of-the-dice-the-river.md" >}})" and closes with a world debut: "[Freehold]({{< relref "2018-12-03-roll-of-the-dice-freehold.md" >}})," which Bruce wrote for the occasion. In between, there's gem after gem: "[Two Hearts]({{< relref "2018-05-28-roll-of-the-dice-two-hearts.md" >}})" and "[When You're Alone]({{< relref "1987-01-11-roll-of-the-dice-when-youre-alone.md" >}})" with Soozie and Patti, "[Open All Night]({{< relref "2020-10-28-roll-of-the-dice-open-all-night.md" >}})" and "[Used Cars]({{< relref "2019-06-13-roll-of-the-dice-used-cars.md" >}})" solo, an absolutely stunning "[Racing in the Street]({{< relref "2021-10-03-roll-of-the-dice-racing-in-the-street.md" >}})" with Soozie, and more. Check out the clips below, and then buy the official archive series release [here](https://live.brucespringsteen.net/live-music/0,19468/Bruce-Springsteen-mp3-flac-download-11-8-1996-St-Rose-of-Lima-School-Freehold-NJ.html).<br>

**2005:** Bruce is in Philadelphia, and you know what that means: fans attending Bruce's solo acoustic show get their traditional surprise treat–this time the long-awaited return of "[Drive All Night,]({{< relref "2018-02-27-roll-of-the-dice-drive-all-night.md" >}})" played for the first time in 24 years. (Watch that moment and other highlights below.) That's not the only tour premiere: Bruce also opens the show with "[Born in the U.S.A.]({{< relref "1984-01-03-roll-of-the-dice-born-in-the-u-s-a.md" >}})" — as a solo *harmonica* number. Not bad for a Night One!<br>

**2009:** Bruce and the E Street Band play the second of two very special full-album shows at Madison Square Garden in New York City. Tonight features a full performance of *The River*, featuring tour premieres of "[Crush on You]({{< relref "2018-02-02-roll-of-the-dice-crush-on-you.md" >}})" (in only its fourth performance since the original River Tour), "Stolen Car" and "[Wreck on the Highway]({{< relref "2022-08-28-roll-of-the-dice-wreck-on-the-highway.md" >}})." The encore features a couple of premieres as well: "[Can't Help Falling in Love]({{< relref "1976-01-21-cover-me-cant-help-falling-in-love.md" >}}/)" and "[Sweet Soul Music]({{< relref "1976-01-20-cover-me-sweet-soul-music.md" >}}/)." This show is available for purchase as part of Bruce's official archive series–get it [here](https://live.brucespringsteen.net/live-music/0,19644/Bruce-Springsteen---The-E-Street-Band-mp3-flac-download-11-8-2009-Madison-Square-Garden-New-York-NY.html). For more highlights from this show, see guest-blogger Marietta Phillips' [*Where the Band Was* report]({{< relref "2019-11-08-where-the-band-was-new-york-city-november-8-2009.md" >}}).<br>
