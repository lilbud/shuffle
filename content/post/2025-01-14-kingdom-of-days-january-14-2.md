**1973:** Bruce and the E Street Band conclude their seven-day, twice-a-day gig at Paul’s Mall in Boston.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/12/1973-01-14.png.webp)

**1980:** At the Power Station in New York City, Bruce and the E Street Band record “Restless Nights.”<br>

**1984**: Bruce joins John Eddie for six songs at the Patrix in New Brunswick, New Jersey.<br>

**2005:** *MTV Unplugged* (the home video release) is certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of America.<br>

{{< youtube-playlist PLJ3gKh8Ty5pbccXmiIkXNfQ2dUMIu6OVT >}}

**2006:** Bruce attends *The Nebraska Project*, a tribute event to the Springsteen album of the same name, at the World Financial Center Winter Garden in New York City. A series of performers cover the tracks from the album; Bruce then joins all the artists for an encore of “[Oklahoma Hills]({{< relref "2021-05-17-matr-bruce-springsteen-arlo-guthrie-joe-ely-and-jimmy-lafave-oklahoma-hills.md" >}})” and explains the true motivation behind the famous album.<br>

**2012:** Bruce makes a “surprise” appearance at the Light of Day benefit in Asbury Park (it’s usually more of a surprise when he *doesn’t* show up), joining Garland Jeffreys for “[Wild in the Streets]({{< relref "2018-04-18-matr-garland-jeffreys-and-bruce-springsteen-wild-in-the-streets.md" >}}),” Willie Nile for “[One Guitar]({{< relref "2018-05-16-meeting-across-the-river-bruce-springsteen-and-willie-nile-one-guitar.md" >}}),” a solo rendition of “[Incident on 57th Street]({{< relref "2018-12-14-roll-of-the-dice-incident-on-57th-street.md" >}}),” and then an entire set with Joe Grushecky and the Houserockers.<br>

**2014:** *High Hopes* is released, Bruce’s eighteenth studio album.<br>

{{< youtube-playlist OLAK5uy_kbLz3tpjFtb1WpZRb5KL6heqhqKuCpuzM >}}

To mark the occasion, Bruce and the E Street Band (+Tom Morello, -Steve Van Zandt) appear on *Late Night With Jimmy Fallon*, playing “[Heaven’s Wall]({{< relref "2020-02-02-roll-of-the-dice-heavens-wall.md" >}})” (the first live performance of that song), and “[Just Like Fire Would.]({{< relref "2020-05-24-cover-me-just-like-fire-would.md" >}})”

Oh, and the first and only performance of “[Gov. Christie Traffic Jam]({{< relref "2022-08-31-matr-rotd-bruce-springsteen-and-jimmy-fallon-gov-christie-traffic-jam.md" >}}).”
