***New for 2025:** Audio for 2008*<br>

**1978:** Bruce debuts his cover of "[Good Rockin' Tonight]({{< relref "2020-09-17-cover-me-good-rockin-tonight.md" >}})" to open Night Two at the Philadelphia Spectrum on the Darkness Tour. Gary Busey guest stars on "[Rave On]({{< relref "2019-03-25-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-gary-busey-rave-on.md" >}})."<br>

{{< youtube oSNz8UoUD4s >}}

**1983:** Bruce guest stars with John Eddie at the Brighton Bar in Long Branch, New Jersey, for a mini-set of covers, including "[Blue Suede Shoes]({{< relref "2020-10-19-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-john-eddie-blue-suede-shoes.md" >}})" and "[Ain't That Lovin' You Baby]({{< relref "2020-12-15-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-john-eddie-aint-that-lovin-you-baby.md" >}})."<br>

{{< videopress id=rAT8zq3b >}}

**1984:** The penultimate night of Bruce's ten-night Born in the U.S.A. Tour stand at East Rutherford's Brendan Byrne Arena brings the U.S. debut of "[My Father's House]({{< relref "2021-08-13-roll-of-the-dice-my-fathers-house.md" >}})" and the return of the Miami Horns for "[Tenth Avenue Freeze-Out]({{< relref "2018-11-19-roll-of-the-dice-tenth-avenue-freeze-out.md" >}})," "[Detroit Medley]({{< relref "1976-01-02-cover-me-detroit-medley.md" >}}/)" and "Twist and Shout." "[Reason to Believe]({{< relref "2021-02-27-roll-of-the-dice-reason-to-believe.md" >}})" is recorded and released on the *Live 1975-85* box set. This show is available as part of Bruce's official archive series–get it [here](https://live.brucespringsteen.net/live-music/0,29593/bruce-springsteen-the-e-street-brendan-byrne-arena-east-rutherford-nj-08-19-1984-mp3-flac-or-online-music-streaming.html).<br>

**1985:** Bruce and the E Street Band play their second of a four-night stand at Giants Stadium (they will return later that summer). The show is notable for its performances of "[Johnny 99,]({{< relref "2020-07-20-roll-of-the-dice-johnny-99.md" >}})" "[Working on the Highway,]({{< relref "1984-01-06-roll-of-the-dice-working-on-the-highway.md" >}})" "[I'm on Fire,]({{< relref "1984-01-08-roll-of-the-dice-im-on-fire.md" >}})" and "Born to Run," all of which are recorded and will be released on the *Live 1975-85* box set. Their "Born to Run" performance will become the audio for the official video for the song.<br>

**2008:** The Magic Tour's victory lap includes a show at Hersheypark Stadium, where Bruce brings back two Tunnel of Love tour staples, both played for the first time in 20 years: "[Boom Boom]({{< relref "2020-03-26-cover-me-boom-boom.md" >}})" and "[Part Man, Part Monkey]({{< relref "2022-02-19-roll-of-the-dice-part-man-part-monkey.md" >}})." The show closes with another tour premiere: "[Gloria]({{< relref "2018-03-16-cover-me-gloria.md" >}})," featuring Joe and Johnny Grushecky (although somehow, the local paper misidentifies Joe for Tommy James).<br>
