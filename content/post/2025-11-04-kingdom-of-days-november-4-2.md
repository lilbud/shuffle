***New for 2025:** Audio for 1976 and 1977; review for 2002 and 2007; new entry for 2024*<br>

**1971:** Tinker West drives Bruce to New York City to introduce him to future manager Mike Appel. Bruce performs a few songs for Appel on piano and acoustic guitar, including "[Song for Orphans]({{< relref "2019-04-10-roll-of-the-dice-song-for-orphans.md" >}})," which Bruce will officially release 49 years later, and "[Baby Doll]({{< relref "2011-01-12-roll-of-the-dice-baby-doll.md" >}})," which fails to impress. Bruce and Appel agree to touch base again someday, but their next meeting won't happen for another four months.<br>

**1975:** Bruce and the E Street Band play their second of three nights at Arizona State University in Tempe.<br>

**1976:** Bruce and the band wrap up a six-night stand at the Palladium in New York City with a barnburner–especially the encores, in which Ronnie Spector joins the band for three of her greatest hits: "[Baby, I Love You]({{< relref "2018-09-02-meeting-across-the-river-bruce-springsteen-and-ronnie-spector-baby-i-love-you.md" >}})," "[Walking in the Rain]({{< relref "2022-01-13-matr-ronnie-spector-and-the-e-street-band-walking-in-the-rain.md" >}})," and "[Be My Baby]({{< relref "2019-06-01-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-ronnie-spector-be-my-baby.md" >}})." All three are live debuts for Bruce and the E Street Band. Also debuting in the encores: Bruce's cover of "[We Gotta Get Out of This Place]({{< relref "2022-03-17-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-eric-burdon-we-gotta-get-out-of-this-place.md" >}})." The main set is pretty great, too, with a particularly inspired "[It's My Life]({{< relref "2021-11-23-cover-me-its-my-life.md" >}})," probably because one of its writers (Carl D'Errico) is in the audience.<br>

**1977:** Bruce takes a break from the recording sessions for [*Darkness on the Edge of Town*]({{< relref "2022-07-05-album-companion-darkness-on-the-edge-of-town.md" >}}) at the Record Plant in New York City to call into radio station WIOQ-FM in Philadelphia, giving DJ Ed Sciaky one of his several interviews with Bruce over the years. Bruce is a bit overly optimistic about the album's release date, though.<br>

{{< youtube yVQZgG2zLgc >}}

At the recording session itself, Bruce works on "[Independence Day]({{< relref "2022-03-14-roll-of-the-dice-independence-day.md" >}})," "[Come On (Let's Go Tonight)]({{< relref "2020-12-05-roll-of-the-dice-come-on-lets-go-tonight.md" >}})," and "[Break Out]({{< relref "2021-03-20-roll-of-the-dice-break-out.md" >}})," the last of which has never been released.

**1978:** Bruce and the E Street Band play a Darkness Tour show at the University of Vermont in Burlington.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/10/1978-11-04.png.webp)

**1984:** Bruce and the band wrap up their seven-night Born in the U.S.A. Tour stand at the L.A. Memorial Sports Arena, where Bruce dedicates "[Shut Out the Light]({{< relref "2020-10-14-roll-of-the-dice-shut-out-the-light.md" >}})" to Ron Kovic (who is in attendance). The official video for "[Born in the U.S.A.]({{< relref "1984-01-03-roll-of-the-dice-born-in-the-u-s-a.md" >}})" is filmed at this show.<br>

**1999:** Patti Scialfa has a perforated eardrum, forcing Bruce to postpone a scheduled Reunion show in Minneapolis.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/10/1999-11-03.png.webp)

**2001:** At the 53rd Annual Emmy Awards, *Live in New York City* is nominated for Outstanding Variety, Music, or Comedy Special but loses to *Cirque du Soleil: Dralion*. Chris Hilson is also nominated as Best Director for *Live in New York City* but loses to David Mallet, the director of *Cirque du Soleil: Dralion.*<br>

**2002:** The Rising Tour moves on to Houston, where Bruce plays a rare solo piano "[For You]({{< relref "2011-01-07-roll-of-the-dice-for-you.md" >}})."<br>

**2009:** Bruce makes a return appearance at the Stand Up for Heroes benefit at Town Hall in New York City, performing songs, telling jokes, and auctioning off a guitar for $50,000.<br>

**2010:** Bruce joins Joe Grushecky and The Houserockers at Soldiers & Sailors Memorial Hall in Pittsburgh for the first of two 15th anniversary shows for *[American Babylon]({{< relref "2020-10-17-american-babylon-a-retrospective.md" >}}).* Bruce opens the show acoustically, with a location-appropriate "[A Good Man is Hard to Find (Pittsburgh)]({{< relref "2021-04-30-roll-of-the-dice-a-good-man-is-hard-to-find-pittsburgh.md" >}})" and two other numbers before joining Joe and the band for a set that includes both Grushecky and Springsteen originals, including the very first performance of "[Save My Love]({{< relref "2019-07-16-roll-of-the-dice-save-my-love.md" >}})."<br>

**2011:** Almost one year to the days, Bruce and Joe Grushecky reprise their pair of Soldiers & Sailors shows in Pittsburgh, with the same format. This is Night Two, and Bruce once again joins opening act The Composure for "[Dancing in the Dark]({{< relref "1984-01-13-roll-of-the-dice-dancing-in-the-dark.md" >}})." An acoustic mini-set follows (featuring "[Incident on 57th Street]({{< relref "2018-12-14-roll-of-the-dice-incident-on-57th-street.md" >}})") and a full set with Joe Grushecky and The Houserockers. But the show doesn't end there–Bruce returns to the stage for a final acoustic set.<br>
