***New for 2025:** New entry for 1975 and 1998*<br>

**1969:** Child plays their second of six consecutive nights at the Pandemonium in Wanamassa, New Jersey.<br>

**1974:** Bruce reschedules a planned gig at The Stone Balloon in Newark, Delaware for the following month.<br>

**1975:** Bruce and the E Street Band complete the recording of "Thunder Road" at the Record Plant in New York City.<br>

**1978:** Bruce and the E Street Band bring the Darkness Tour to the New Orleans Municipal Auditorium, where the setlist features Bruce's one and only cover of "[Honky Tonk]({{< relref "2019-09-26-cover-me-honky-tonk.md" >}})."<br>

**1979:** At The Power Station in New York City, Bruce records "[Ricky Wants a Man of Her Own]({{< relref "2018-11-23-roll-of-the-dice-ricky-wants-a-man-of-her-own.md" >}})" and "[Cindy]({{< relref "2018-08-03-roll-of-the-dice-cindy.md" >}})." The former won't be released for almost twenty years, the latter not for another fifteen years after that.<br>

**1981:** Bruce's five-night River stand at The Spectrum in Philly continues.<br>

{{< videopress id=8V1HMlo9 >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/07/1981-07-16.jpg.webp)

**1983:** Bruce guest-stars with Midnight Thunder for six songs at The Headliner in Neptune, New Jersey, including "[Lucille]({{< relref "1976-01-07-cover-me-lucille.md" >}}/)," "[Wooly Bully]({{< relref "2018-03-31-cover-me-woolly-bully.md" >}})," and "[Keep-a-Knockin]({{< relref "2018-11-09-matr-bruce-springsteen-midnight-thunder-and-southside-johnny-keep-knockin.md" >}})'."<br>

{{< videopress id=rSVwCU6r >}}

**1984:** On a night off in between Born in the U.S.A. shows in Chicago, Bruce is spotted playing pinball at a local bar.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/07/1984-07-16.png.webp)

**1994:** Bruce (along with Jon Bon Jovi, Patti, and Max) celebrates the 20th anniversary of The Stone Pony by joining Southside Johnny on stage for seven songs, including "[I Played the Fool]({{< relref "2019-04-04-matr-i-played-the-fool.md" >}})," "[Funky Broadway,]({{< relref "2019-10-28-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-the-shots-funky-broadway.md" >}})" and "[Jersey Girl]({{< relref "1976-02-07-cover-me-roll-of-the-dice-jersey-girl.md" >}}/)."<br>

**1998:** At a Sony trade show in Miami, Bruce announces an upcoming box set of unreleased tracks.<br>

**2002:** Bruce and the E Street Band rehearse for the upcoming Rising Tour.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2019/07/rehearsal.jpg.webp)

**2005:** The Devils & Dust Tour returns to the U.S., and the first show of the leg is at the Pepsi Arena in Albany. Bruce tour premieres "[Does This Bus Stop at 82nd Street]({{< relref "2011-01-04-roll-of-the-dice-does-this-bus-stop-at-82nd-street.md" >}}/)" for the occasion.<br>

**2013:** Bruce opens his show in Limerick with the tour premiere of "[This Little Light of Mine]({{< relref "1976-01-31-cover-me-this-little-light-of-mine.md" >}}/)" and closes it the same way. In between, fans enjoy the tour premiere of "[Ain't Too Proud to Beg](../2018-10-07-cover-me-aint-too-proud-to-beg/post.md/)" and a full album performance of *Born to Run*. If you count a three-song pre-show, this show also features the live debut of "[Hearts of Stone]({{< relref "2022-09-07-roll-of-the-dice-hearts-of-stone.md" >}})" at one of Bruce's own shows.<br>

**2016:** A stunning opening number at a magical venue: The Roma Sinfonietta strings join the E Street Band for "New York City Serenade" to kick off Bruce's first show at the ancient Circus Maximus in Rome. "[Summertime Blues]({{< relref "2020-01-02-cover-me-summertime-blues.md" >}})" and "[The Ghost of Tom Joad]({{< relref "2019-10-17-roll-of-the-dice-the-ghost-of-tom-joad.md" >}})" also make their tour premieres. For more highlights from this show, see my [*Where the Band Was*]({{< relref "2018-07-16-where-the-band-was-rome-july-16-2016.md" >}})[report]({{< relref "2018-07-16-where-the-band-was-rome-july-16-2016.md" >}}) from this night.<br>
