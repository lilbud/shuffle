***New for 2025:** New entry for 1977, 1979, and 2024; audio for 2008*<br>

**1971:** The Sundance Blues Band plays a gig at the Student Prince in Asbury Park.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/06/1971-0627.jpg.webp)

**1972:** It's a big day in the studio for Bruce: he records "[For You]({{< relref "2011-01-07-roll-of-the-dice-for-you.md" >}})," "[Growin' Up]({{< relref "2011-01-03-roll-of-the-dice-growin-up.md" >}}/)," "[Lost in the Flood]({{< relref "2011-01-05-roll-of-the-dice-lost-in-the-flood.md" >}}/)," "[Does This Bus Stop at 82nd Street]({{< relref "2011-01-04-roll-of-the-dice-does-this-bus-stop-at-82nd-street.md" >}}/)," "Visitation at Fort Horn," "[Lady and the Doctor]({{< relref "2011-01-21-roll-of-the-dice-lady-and-the-doctor.md" >}}/)," and "[Arabian Nights]({{< relref "2011-01-11-roll-of-the-dice-arabian-nights.md" >}})," the last three of which remain unreleased to this day.<br>

**1974:** A scheduled E Street Band gig in Memphis is cancelled.<br>

**1977:** Bruce and the E Street Band work on material for what will become *Darkness on the Edge of Town* at Atlantic Studios in New York City.<br>

**1978:** [*Darkness on the Edge of Town*]({{< relref "2022-07-05-album-companion-darkness-on-the-edge-of-town.md" >}}) is certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America.<br>

{{< youtube-playlist OLAK5uy_lDjjM6fhv8UAF1WLd5MyGr39PsIy3DXjs >}}

**1979:** Bruce and the E Street Band record the backing track for "[The Time That Never Was]({{< relref "2022-08-25-roll-of-the-dice-the-time-that-never-was.md" >}})" at the Power Station in New York City. Bruce will finally complete it 36 years later and release it that same year.<br>

**1982:** Bruce performs "[Tenth Avenue Freeze-Out]({{< relref "2018-11-19-roll-of-the-dice-tenth-avenue-freeze-out.md" >}})" with Clarence and the Red Bank Rockers at Big Man's West, and then he zips over to The Stone Pony to play six songs with Cats on a Smooth Surface, including the world premiere of "From Small Things (Big Things One Day Come)." Listen to the soundboard-sourced show below.<br>

{{< youtube I0YWV3aIaqw >}}

**1983:** Bruce records "[None But the Brave]({{< relref "2018-09-24-roll-of-the-dice-none-but-the-brave.md" >}})" at The Hit Factory in New York City.<br>

{{< youtube j6Z2l9iBMkQ >}}

**1994:** Bruce gets on stage at the West Hollywood House of Blues and performs six songs with Sacred Hearts, Danny Federici's post-E Street band.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/06/1994-06-27.jpg.webp)

**1999:** Bruce and the E Street Band wrap up the European leg of their Reunion Tour at Valle Hovin Stadion in Oslo.<br>

**2005:** Bruce tour premieres "[Janey, Don't You Lose Heart]({{< relref "2019-04-06-roll-of-the-dice-janey-dont-you-lose-heart.md" >}})," "[Spare Parts]({{< relref "1987-01-04-roll-of-the-dice-spare-parts.md" >}}/)," and "[Growin' Up]({{< relref "2011-01-03-roll-of-the-dice-growin-up.md" >}}/)" at his solo acoustic show at Hamburg's Color Line Arena. As he often does on this tour, Bruce stops to meet fans and sign autographs before the show.<br>

**2008:** Elliott Murphy and his son guest star when the Magic Tour stops at Parc des Princes in Paris. The setlist features a rare outing of "Janey, Don't You Lose Heart."<br>

**2009:** Bruce headlines the Glastonbury Festival in England, but first he joins The Gaslight Anthem during their set for "[The '59 Sound]({{< relref "2018-01-27-meeting-across-the-river-gaslight-anthem-and-bruce-springsteen-the-59-sound.md" >}})."<br>

Bruce opens his own set with his first and only performance of "[Coma Girl,]({{< relref "2019-03-13-cover-me-coma-girl.md" >}})" in tribute to Joe Strummer. Brian Fallon returns Bruce's favor from earlier in the day by guest-starring on "[No Surrender]({{< relref "1984-01-09-roll-of-the-dice-no-surrender.md" >}})."
