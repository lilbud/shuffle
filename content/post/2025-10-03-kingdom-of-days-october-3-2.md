***New for 2025:** New entry for 2024*<br>

**1971:** The Bruce Springsteen Band is in residence at the Student Prince in Asbury Park.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/10/1971-1001.jpg.webp)

**1975:** A scheduled show at the Rivoli Theatre in Indianapolis is cancelled.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2023/10/medium.jpg.webp)

**1976:** Bruce and the E Street Band continue their week of California shows with a stop at Santa Clara University.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/09/1976-10-03.jpg.webp)

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/10/1976-1003.jpg.webp)

**1980:** The original River Tour kicks off at the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, with "Born to Run" opening the show for the first time ever. Bruce is clearly a bit rusty, as he forgets the lyrics to his most well-known song. "Thunder Road" is played twice, the first time mid-set and the second as a final encore with Bob Seger.<br>

**2003:** A stunning penultimate show for the Rising Tour at Shea Stadium from start to finish: Bruce opens with the tour premiere of "[Roulette]({{< relref "2022-04-16-roll-of-the-dice-roulette.md" >}})," plays another double-header of tour premieres mid-set ("[Rendezvous]({{< relref "2021-05-08-roll-of-the-dice-rendezvous.md" >}})" and "Another Thin Line"), along with rare outings for "[Janey, Don't You Lose Heart]({{< relref "2019-04-06-roll-of-the-dice-janey-dont-you-lose-heart.md" >}})" and "New York City Serenade" (another tour premiere). Willie Nile and Gary U.S. Bonds guest-star during the encores, and Al Franken makes a guest appearance during the main set.<br>

**2004:** The Vote for Change Tour moves on to the next swing state–Michigan–with a show at Cobo Arena. "[The Ties That Bind]({{< relref "2018-03-09-roll-of-the-dice-the-ties-that-bind.md" >}})" and "[Darkness on the Edge of Town]({{< relref "2016-01-07-roll-of-the-dice-darkness-on-the-edge-of-town.md" >}})" replace "[The River]({{< relref "2021-03-04-roll-of-the-dice-the-river.md" >}})" in the set, and "Travelin' Band" makes its debut in the encores. The Dixie Chicks are in town for their VfC show, too, and they make it over to the Cobo in time to join Bruce and the assembled artists for the final encore. For more highlights from this show, see my [Where the Band Was report]({{< relref "2018-10-03-where-the-band-was-detroit-october-3-2004.md" >}}).<br>

**2005:** While driving his kids to school, Bruce notices on the radio that his favorite local public station is holding a membership drive. The station makes a plea for community support, so Bruce decides to provide it, stopping by the station with his guitar and spending over thirty minutes on-air talking with their music director and playing two songs. One of them, ["If I Should Fall Behind]({{< relref "1992-02-05-roll-of-the-dice-if-i-should-fall-behind.md" >}})," will be released on a promotional CD for pledging members.<br>

**2006:** In an unusual move, Bruce re-releases *[We Shall Overcome: The Seeger Sessions]({{< relref "2006-01-01-album-companion-we-shall-overcome.md" >}})* in the same year as its original release, but this time as an *American Land Edition*, featuring five additional tracks and new videos.<br>

**2007:** *Live in Dublin* (the video release, not the album) is certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of America.<br>

**2009:** Night Three of five at Giants Stadium on the Working on a Dream Tour features the entire *Born in the U.S.A.* album along with the tour premiere of "Last to Die" and a by-request "[Jersey Girl.]({{< relref "1976-02-07-cover-me-roll-of-the-dice-jersey-girl.md" >}}/)" Jeremy Chatzky joins the band and Curt Ramm for "[American Land]({{< relref "2016-01-22-roll-of-the-dice-american-land.md" >}})," and the set list continues to benefit from the presence of Curt and his trumpet.<br>

**2016:** Bruce makes an appearance at Barnes & Noble at The Grove in Los Angeles to promote his new autobiography.<br>

{{< youtube odKtPqBfo-U >}}

**2017:** Bruce makes his official Broadway debut when *Springsteen on Broadway* opens in its first public preview performance at the Walter Kerr Theater in New York City. Bruce dedicates this first show to Tom Petty, who passed away the day before. The show itself is an emotional tour through Bruce's life and work, stunning those present with its power, poignancy, heart and humor–all of which will grow throughout its long run. I'm in the front row for this historic performance–read my account of the evening in my *[Where the Band Was report]({{< relref "2018-10-03-where-the-man-was-broadway-october-3-2017.md" >}})*. Also in attendance tonight: Little Steven, Bruce's mom, and Bruce's sister Pam.<br>

**2022:** Bruce films videos for "Nightshift" and "[Turn Back the Hands of Time]({{< relref "2024-07-04-cover-me-turn-back-the-hands-of-time.md" >}})" at Samson Stages in Brooklyn.<br>

**2024:** On social media, Bruce publishes his endorsement of Vice President Kamala Harris in the 2024 U.S. presidential election campaign. Although the endorsement itself was unsurprising, the passion and urgency of Bruce's plea sustains for days through the news cycle.<br>
