**1961:** Eleven-year-old Bruce Springsteen receives the sacrament of confirmation at St. Rose of Lima Church in Freehold.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/05/1961-05-05.jpg.webp)

**1973:** Bruce and the as yet unnamed E Street Band play a show at Providence College in Rhode Island.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/04/1973-0505.jpg.webp)

**1974:** Bruce postpones a scheduled concert at Kent State University to June 1.<br>

**1975:** At a *Born to Run* recording session at the Record Plant in New York City, Bruce begins work on “Tenth Avenue Freeze-Out.”<br>

**1981:** Bruce plays his first-ever show in Norway, when the River Tour comes to Drammen.<br>

{{< videopress id=fZwe49y9 >}}

**1982:** At The Power Station studio in New York City, Bruce and the E Street Band record “Glory Days,” “My Love Will Not Let You Down,” “A Good Man Is Hard to Find (Pittsburgh)” and the still unreleased “Stop the War.”<br>

**2006:** The first European leg of the Seeger Sessions Tour kicks off at the Point Theater in Dublin with the tour debuts of “Adam Raised a Cain” and “Erie Canal.”<br>

{{< videopress id=kPchYwxD >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2024/05/The_Independent_Sat__May_6__2006_.jpg.webp)

**2009:** Bruce plays his fourth show in four days, as the Working on a Dream Tour rolls into Charlottesville, Virginia, for a show at the John Paul Jones arena. The set list includes a one-off cover (by request) of The Kinks’ “You Really Got Me,” along with the tour premieres of “Gypsy Biker” and “Roulette.”<br>

{{< youtube -MMryww4Ae8 >}}

**2018:** *Springsteen on Broadway* continues its theatrical run at the Walter Kerr Theater in New York City.<br>

**2019:** Bruce appears at the opening night of Netflix’s third annual FYSEE event, playing two songs and joining director Martin Scorsese for a Q&A session.<br>

**2023:** Bruce kicks off a three-night stand in Dublin without any tour premieres, the first debut-less show since March. The encores open with “Land of Hope and Dreams,” dedicated to Charlie Bird, who is in attendance tonight.<br>

{{< youtube GqK9Q3Dq5B8 >}}

**2024:** Bruce and the E Street Band kick off the European leg of their World Tour with the tour debut of “So Young and In Love” at Principality Stadium in Cardiff. Also tour premiering tonight by request: “Better Days.”<br>
