**1970:** Steel Mill opens for Boz Scaggs at The Matrix in San Francisco.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/02/1972-02-14.jpg.webp)

**1971:** Bruce jams at an open mic night at The Upstage in Asbury Park.<br>

**1972:** The Bruce Springsteen Band opens for Crazy Horse at the Sunshine In in Asbury Park.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2025/02/Asbury_Park_Press_1972_02_02_Page_22-scaled.jpg.webp)

**1974:** Bruce and the still unnamed E Street Band play the University of Kentucky, their first time performing in the state. After a fight with road manager Steve Appel, drummer Vini Lopez is fired immediately after the show, and the next three shows are cancelled.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/02/1974-02-12.jpg.webp)

**1977:** The Lawsuit Tour continues with a show at the Ottawa Civic Center.<br>

**1981:** The original River Tour stops in Mobile, Alabama.<br>

{{< videopress id=lJzCY3iA >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2024/02/Birmingham_Post_Herald_Fri__Feb_13__1981_.jpg.webp)

**1990**: Bruce, Sting, Bruce Hornsby, and others attend and perform at a benefit for the Rainforest Foundation in Beverly Hills. Among the numbers Bruce performs: his only known cover of “Big Yellow Taxi.”<br>

After the show, Bruce, Sting, Don Henley, Herbie Hancock, Branford Marsalis, and Joe Walsh hit the China Club in Hollywood and jam on stage.

**1996:** Bruce begins his first European solo acoustic tour in Frankfurt.<br>

{{< youtube qVfDA38YmwE >}}

**1997:** Bruce’s marathon solo acoustic tour continues with a fifth and final show at the Capital Theater in Sydney, Australia.<br>

{{< videopress id=pbK3YKjI >}}

**1999:** Bruce records audio and video for “[Give My Love to Rose]({{< relref "2020-10-29-cover-me-give-my-love-to-rose.md" >}})” for a Johnny Cash tribute special. It will be broadcast in April.<br>

{{< youtube rF2ikBJbV5U >}}

**2012:** Bruce and the E Street Band open the Grammy Awards with the live debut of “[We Take Care of Our Own.]({{< relref "2020-12-25-roll-of-the-dice-we-take-care-of-our-own.md" >}})” At the end of the show, Bruce joins an epic jam with Paul McCartney, Dave Grohl, and Joe Walsh on “[The End.]({{< relref "2024-07-06-matr-paul-mccartney-and-bruce-springsteen-the-end.md" >}})” Watch both performances and behind-the-scenes footage below.<br>

**2014:** Night Two in Adelaide is a scorcher… literally. It was a hot day, and it prompts Bruce to open with a cover of “[Heat Wave]({{< relref "2020-02-18-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-martha-reeves-heat-wave.md" >}}).” This show also features the first and only (to date) performance of “[Hunter of Invisible Game]({{< relref "2022-01-18-roll-of-the-dice-hunter-of-invisible-game.md" >}}).”<br>
