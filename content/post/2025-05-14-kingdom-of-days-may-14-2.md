***New for 2025:** New entry for 1973, 1979, and 1982; audio for 1993; video for 2016*<br>

**1971:** Dr. Zoom and the Sonic Boom make their debut (and penultimate) performance under that name, at Asbury Park's Sunshine In.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/05/1971-05-14.jpg.webp)

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/05/1971-0514.jpg.webp)

**1973:** Bruce assembles his band at 914 Sound Studio to begin recording his second album.<br>

**1974:** Bruce cancels a scheduled gig at Tusculum College in Greeneville, Tennessee, to make room for recording sessions for *Born to Run*.<br>

**1979:** Bruce rehearses with the E Street Band at his home studio in Holmdel. Listen to their rehearsal of "[Bring on the Night]({{< relref "2018-05-23-roll-of-the-dice-bring-on-the-night.md" >}})" below.<br>

{{< youtube 48hBjV19GlM >}}

**1981:** Those poor ticket holders for Bruce's second River Tour show in Manchester, England: The original concert was postponed from March 24th when Bruce took ill, and on this night, Bruce is sick again. He performs an abbreviated set (five songs fewer than the night before) at the Apollo Theatre, the shortest show of the European leg.<br>

{{< videopress id=oAGgq6zb >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/05/1981-0514.jpg.webp)

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2024/05/Daily_Mail_Thu__May_21__1981_-scaled.jpg.webp)

**1982:** Bruce and the E Street Band are in the studio at the Power Station in New York City, working on what will become *Born in the U.S.A.*<br>

**1985:** Bruce and Julianne hold their wedding reception, a day after their wedding.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/05/1985-05-14.jpg.webp)

**1988:** On *American Top 40*, host Casey Kasem introduces Natalie Cole's cover of "[Pink Cadillac]({{< relref "2019-08-21-roll-of-the-dice-pink-cadillac.md" >}})" with a medley of Springsteen covers that hit the Top 40.<br>

[https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/AT40-1988-05-14.mp3](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/AT40-1988-05-14.mp3)

**1993:** Bruce's World Tour 1993 moves on to Germany for the first of four shows. The opener is in Berlin, where Bruce plays "[Satan's Jeweled Crown]({{< relref "2020-05-09-cover-me-satans-jeweled-crown.md" >}})" for the very first time. This show is available as part of Bruce's official archive series–get it [here](https://live.brucespringsteen.net/live-music/0,28724/Bruce-Springsteen-mp3-flac-download-5-14-1993-Waldb%C3%BChne-Berlin-DE.html).<br>

**2016:** The European leg of the River Tour kicks off with a show at Camp Nou in Barcelona. Immediately noticeable: Bruce is no longer playing the entire *The River* album, although he does play much of it, along with a rare outing for "[I Wanna Be With You]({{< relref "2018-01-14-roll-of-the-dice-i-wanna-be-with-you.md" >}})" and the tour premiere of "Twist and Shout."
