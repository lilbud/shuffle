**1973:** Bruce and his band play their second of six nights (with two shows each night) at Max’s Kansas City in New York, opening for Biff Rose.<br>

**1974:** Bruce and the band open for Wishbone Ash at Cleveland’s Allen Theater.<br>

**1981:** The original River Tour stops at the St. Paul Civic Center Arena. As a fan explains to a reviewer: It’s not all Twang! Twang! Boing!<br>

**1986:** “[My Hometown]({{< relref "1983-01-12-roll-of-the-dice-my-hometown.md" >}})” tops the Billboard Adult Contemporary chart for one week. Bruce will never have another #1 hit on this chart.<br>

**2009**: Step back from the guacamole dip, put the chicken fingers *down*, and turn your television ALL the way up: Bruce Springsteen and the E Street Band play the Super Bowl XLIII Half-Time Show! This is the full-band premiere of “[Working on a Dream.]({{< relref "2019-07-04-roll-of-the-dice-working-on-a-dream.md" >}})” Watch Bruce’s journal, interview, and full performance below.<br>

**2014:** Bruce and the E Street Band finish their mini-South African tour at FNB Stadium in Johannesburg. Shortly after doors open, Bruce surprises the sparse crowd with a three-song acoustic pre-show. Two of the three songs (“[I’ll Work for Your Love]({{< relref "2018-02-14-roll-of-the-dice-ill-work-for-your-love.md" >}})” and “[Growin’ Up]({{< relref "2011-01-03-roll-of-the-dice-growin-up.md" >}}/)“) are captured and posted on Bruce’s official YouTube channel. Meanwhile, *High Hopes* becomes Bruce’s 11th #1 album in the U.S., although it will only hold that spot for one week. “[Seeds]({{< relref "2020-10-10-roll-of-the-dice-seeds.md" >}}),” [Cadillac Ranch]({{< relref "2020-07-23-roll-of-the-dice-cadillac-ranch.md" >}}),” “[I’m on Fire]({{< relref "1984-01-08-roll-of-the-dice-im-on-fire.md" >}}),” and “[Who’ll Stop the Rain]({{< relref "2023-07-12-cover-me-wholl-stop-the-rain.md" >}})” all make their tour premieres tonight.<br>

**2018:** *Springsteen on Broadway* continues its theatrical run at the Walter Kerr Theater in New York City.<br>

**2023:** Bruce and the E Street Band take the stage for the first time in six years, kicking off their world tour in Tampa, Florida. “[Letter to You,]({{< relref "2017-01-03-roll-of-the-dice-letter-to-you.md" >}})” “Nighshift,” “[Don’t Play That Song]({{< relref "2023-02-12-cover-me-dont-play-that-song-you-lied.md" >}}),” and “[House of a Thousand Guitars]({{< relref "2016-01-15-roll-of-the-dice-house-of-a-thousand-guitars.md" >}})” all make their E Street Band debuts, and “[Burnin’ Train]({{< relref "2022-01-14-roll-of-the-dice-burnin-train.md" >}})” makes its live debut.<br>
