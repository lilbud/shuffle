**1972:** The Bruce Springsteen Band plays their seventh show this month at The Back Door in Richmond. This show features the only known circulating performance of “[Down to the Riverside]({{< relref "1972-02-05-roll-of-the-dice-down-to-the-riverside.md" >}}).”<br>

**1974:** Sick with the flu, Bruce somehow manages to perform at The Main Point in Bryn Mawr, Pennsylvania, but he is forced to sit for the entire performance. Even though he’s sick, a photo from this evening will become the cover of *Tracks* 24 years later. (photo credits: Phil Ceccola)<br>

**1981:** The River Tour makes a stop at the Mid-South Coliseum in Memphis. Meanwhile, Bruce loses the Best Rock Vocal Performance (Male) Grammy Award to Billy Joel’s *Glass Houses*. (Bruce was nominated for “[Detroit Medley]({{< relref "1976-01-02-cover-me-detroit-medley.md" >}}/).”)<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2024/02/The_Memphis_Press_Scimitar_Thu__Feb_26__1981_-scaled.jpg.webp)

**1986:** Grammy-nominated “[Born in the U.S.A.]({{< relref "1984-01-03-roll-of-the-dice-born-in-the-u-s-a.md" >}})” loses the Record of the Year award to “[We Are the World]({{< relref "2022-01-30-matr-bruce-springsteen-and-just-about-everyone-we-are-the-world.md" >}})” (which at least features Bruce’s vocals).<br>

{{< youtube 9AjkUyX0rVw >}}

**1988:** The Tunnel of Love Express Tour kicks off in Worcester, Massachusetts. Most *[Tunnel of Love]({{< relref "1987-01-01-album-companion-tunnel-of-love.md" >}})*songs receive their first concert airings, and old standards are played with new arrangements. Unheard outtakes and covers round out a tightly thematic set list and a very different Springsteen show than most fans have ever heard. The Miami Horns join the E Street Band for this tour, temporarily renamed The Horns of Love.<br>

**1996:** Bruce plays a solo acoustic show in Rotterdam.<br>

{{< videopress id=Mn7BVXOQ >}}

**1998:** Nominated for the Best Male Rock Vocal Performance Grammy for “Thunder Road,” Bruce loses to Bob Dylan’s “Cold Irons Bound.”<br>

**2000:** Four New York City police officers are acquitted of all charges in the shooting death of Amadou Diallo, an immigrant from West Africa who was shot at 41 times by the police even though he was unarmed. Bruce will shortly write a song about it and perform it to much controversy in New York at the end of the Reunion Tour.<br>

**2005:** Bruce wins the Grammy Award for Best Solo Rock Vocal Performance for “[Code of Silence]({{< relref "2018-08-27-codeofsilence.md" >}}).”<br>

{{< youtube qmliFFBFQAQ >}}
