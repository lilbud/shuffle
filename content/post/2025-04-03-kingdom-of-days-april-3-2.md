***New for 2025:** New entry for 1978; review for 2009; video for2023*<br>

**1969:** Child continues their opening stand at the Pandemonium in Wanamassa, New Jersey.<br>

**1971:** Bruce and Steve play The Upstage in Asbury Park, leading separate bands but each playing a slate of hits from the 1950s.<br>

**1978:** At the Record Plant in New York City, Bruce and his studio team complete the mix for "[Factory]({{< relref "2018-10-31-roll-of-the-dice-factory.md" >}})."<br>

{{< youtube 3YuEJ_XolkM >}}

**1979:** Bruce records multiple takes of "[Roulette]({{< relref "2022-04-16-roll-of-the-dice-roulette.md" >}})," just six days after the Three Mile Island accident that inspired it.<br>

{{< youtube _QWRFI3M0mo >}}

**1981:** Bruce postpones a scheduled show in London to recover from the previous leg of the River Tour.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2018/03/1981-0318.jpg.webp)

**1985:** Bruce and the E Street Band play their first of two *Born in the U.S.A.* shows in Melbourne, Australia. Bruce performs "[Johnny Bye-Bye]({{< relref "1983-01-05-roll-of-the-dice-johnny-bye-bye.md" >}})" for the final time to date.<br>

{{< videopress id=9ND97yT2 >}}

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2024/04/The_Age_Thu__Apr_4__1985_.jpg.webp)

**1988:** *The Asbury Park Press* spotlights Los Angeles outposts for Springsteen fans.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/04/1988-04-03-1.jpg.webp)

**1993:** Bruce brings his World Tour 1993 to Dortmund, Germany, where he debuts his cover of "[Many Rivers to Cross]({{< relref "2018-07-28-cover-me-many-rivers-to-cross.md" >}})" and Jon Bon Jovi and Richie Sambora join him on stage for "[Glory Days]({{< relref "1984-01-12-roll-of-the-dice-glory-days.md" >}})."<br>

{{< videopress id=fUeXizaZ >}}

**2000:** The Reunion Tour hits the Pacific Northwest, starting with a single show at Portland's Rose Garden that features the tour premiere of "[Dead Man Walkin]({{< relref "2018-07-24-roll-of-the-dice-dead-man-walkin.md" >}})'."<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2024/04/The_Columbian_Tue__Apr_4__2000_-1.jpg.webp)

**2001:** *Live in New York City* is released, documenting the E Street Band's Reunion Tour. The album goes on sale shortly after midnight, and Bruce makes a surprise visit to Jack's Music in Red Bank to kick off the festivities and sign autographs for the first buyers.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2020/04/2001-04-03a.jpg.webp)

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2021/04/2001-04-03b-scaled.jpg.webp)

**2009:** In Glendale, Night Two on the Working on a Dream Tour brings six set list change-ups (including "[Night]({{< relref "2019-10-31-roll-of-the-dice-night.md" >}})," "[Working on the Highway]({{< relref "1984-01-06-roll-of-the-dice-working-on-the-highway.md" >}})," and "[Downbound Train]({{< relref "1984-01-07-roll-of-the-dice-downbound-train.md" >}})"), rare for this early in the tour.<br>

![](https://media.githubusercontent.com/media/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/master/assets_webp/2025/04/El_Paso_Times_2009_04_07_22.jpg.webp)

**2012:** The Wrecking Ball Tour stops at the Izod Center in East Rutherford for the first of two nights. The highlight: the tour premiere of "So Young and In Love."<br>
